# Topics Research: Did Jephthah Sacrifice His Daughter, or Dedicate Her to Perpetual Virginity?

## Question
Did Jephthah actually sacrifice his daughter as a burnt offering (Judges 11:29-40), or did he dedicate her to perpetual virginity / lifelong temple service? What does the Hebrew text, the grammar of the vow, and the surrounding biblical theology indicate?

---

## Topics (Nave's Topical Dictionary — full entries organized by theme)

### A. The Narrative of Jephthah's Vow

#### JEPHTHAH
(A judge — leader, hero — of Israel)
- Illegitimate, not entitled to inherit father's property: **Jdg 11:1, 2**
- Escapes half-brothers' violence, lives in Tob: **Jdg 11:3**
- Recalled from Tob by elders of Gilead: **Jdg 11:5**
- Made captain of the army: **Jdg 11:5-11**
- Made head of Gilead: **Jdg 11:7-11**
- Diplomatic message to king of Ammon (Torah-literate argument): **Jdg 11:12-28**
- Leads Israel against Ammon (Spirit of YHWH upon him): **Jdg 11:29-33**
- His rash vow concerning his daughter: **Jdg 11:31, 34-40**
- Falsely accused by Ephraimites: **Jdg 12:1**
- Leads Gileadites against Ephraim (Shibboleth): **Jdg 12:4**
- Judges Israel six years, dies, buried in Gilead: **Jdg 12:7**
- Faith of: **Heb 11:32**

*Nave's editorial note:* Nave classifies Jdg 11:31, 34-40 explicitly under "rash vow" — this is an interpretive choice consistent with the literal-sacrifice reading, not a Scriptural verdict.

### G. Human Sacrifice (Categorically Prohibited)

#### MOLECH (Also MOLOCH, MILCOM — Idol of Ammon)
- An idol of the Ammonites: **Acts 7:43** (parallel 1 Kgs 11:5 "Milcom the abomination of the Ammonites")
- Worshiped by Solomon's wives, and by Solomon: 1 Kgs 11:1-8
- **Children sacrificed to Molech — the entire prohibition-and-condemnation corpus:**
  - 2 Kgs 23:10 (Josiah defiles Topheth)
  - Jer 32:35 (Hinnom sacrifices condemned)
  - 2 Kgs 16:3 (Ahaz "made his son to pass through the fire")
  - 2 Kgs 21:6 (Manasseh)
  - 2 Chr 28:3 (Ahaz — parallel)
  - Isa 57:5 (slaying children in valleys)
  - **Jer 7:31** ⬅ divine disavowal: "which I commanded them not, neither came it into my heart"
  - Ezek 16:20-21 ("slain my children")
  - Ezek 20:26, 31 ("pass through fire")
  - Ezek 23:37, 39 (sacrificed "to be devoured")
- **See Lev 18:21; 20:2-5** (Pentateuchal prohibition — death penalty for giving seed to Molech).

*Critical observation:* Molech was the idol **of Ammon specifically** — the very nation Jephthah was fighting (Jdg 11). Under the literal-sacrifice reading, Jephthah would be **reproducing the exact abomination of the enemy god** immediately after defeating them — a theological grotesquerie that deepens rather than solves the problem.

#### BURNT OFFERING → OFFERINGS, BURNT
(The olah technical category; normally animal — bullock, ram, lamb, kid, bird; Lev 1:3-17; Num 28-29 daily/festival schedule. Human immolation as burnt-offering appears in Scripture only at: Gen 22 Akedah — ARRESTED; 2 Kgs 3:27 — Mesha the Moabite king, contrary to YHWH, producing "great indignation upon Israel"; and as the prophets' catalog of condemned Molech-worship. The literal reading of Jdg 11:39 would place Jephthah in a category occupied only by pagan enemies and by the Akedah-test — uniquely applied to a voluntary Israelite daughter.)
