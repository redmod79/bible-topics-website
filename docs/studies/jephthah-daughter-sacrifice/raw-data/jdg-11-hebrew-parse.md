unambiguous disjunctive particle אוֹ ('o) but Jdg 11:31 does NOT use it. Neither LXX
A-text (καί) nor B-text (asyndeton) read disjunction.

CRITICAL OBSERVATIONS ON JDG 11:39:
1. וַיַּעַשׂ לָהּ אֶת־נִדְרוֹ — "he did to her his vow." The narrator uses colorless
   עשׂה ("do/make") + dative construction, avoiding:
   - H2076 זבח (slaughter/sacrifice) — absent
   - H7819 שׁחט (slaughter technically) — absent
   - H8313 שׂרף (burn) — absent
   - H5927 Hiphil עלה ("offer up") + עלה ("burnt offering") — absent, though
     this is exactly the verb-cluster Jephthah himself used in 11:31 AND that
     2 Kgs 3:27 uses of Mesha's identical act ("wayya'alehu olah").
   - הֶעֱבִיר בָּאֵשׁ (pass through fire) — absent
   The lexical restraint is conspicuous against every canonical control for
   reported human immolation (see raw-data/verse-list.txt). The narrator names
   the act only by reference back to the vow-utterance, not by sacrificial verb.

2. וְהִיא לֹא־יָדְעָה אִישׁ — WX-QATAL (waw + fronted subject-pronoun + qatal).
   This is NOT a wayyiqtol sequential clause. It BREAKS the wayyiqtol chain
   between וַיַּעַשׂ and וַתְּהִי. WX-QATAL signals circumstantial / backgrounded
   / stative-resultant information (W-O'C §39.2.3; GKC §156).
   Grammatical verdict from library entry (syntax-wx-qatal-circumstantial.md):
   "The clause is NOT a statement that her virginity continued after the vow's
   execution through a subsequent lifetime of celibacy (which would be the
   sequential reading); it is a background state-description framing the
   fulfillment of the vow."
   Whatever else it means, it is the narrator's interpretive gloss on "doing
   the vow" expressed in SEXUAL/MARITAL terms, not mortality terms.

3. Feminine-subject + ידע + אישׁ euphemism: Gen 24:16 Rebekah (alive); Num
   31:17-18, 35 Midianite women (alive or killed — explicitly spelled out);
   Jdg 21:12 Jabesh-Gilead virgins (alive); 1 Kgs 1:4 Abishag (alive) —
   EVERY OT occurrence of the feminine "lo yada ish" formula describes LIVING
   women's sexual status. No OT occurrence describes a dead woman.

=== Judges 11:40 — Annual Rite ===
מִיָּמִים יָמִימָה תֵּלַכְנָה בְּנוֹת יִשְׂרָאֵל לְתַנּוֹת לְבַת־יִפְתָּח הַגִּלְעָדִי אַרְבַּעַת יָמִים בַּשָּׁנָה

KEY: לְתַנּוֹת (Verb.Piel.InfCon תנה) — dis legomenon; ONLY other OT occurrence
     is Jdg 5:11 יְתַנּוּ צִדְקוֹת יְהוָה — "they recount/rehearse the righteous
     acts of the LORD" (CELEBRATIVE). LXX splits: Jdg 5:11 → διηγέομαι
     "recount"; Jdg 11:40 → θρηνεῖν "lament" — two different tonal renderings
     of one Hebrew verb.
KEY: ל + בַת־יִפְתָּח — dative "TO/FOR the daughter" — ל of interest/benefit,
     not עַל of object-of-mourning. Contrast בכה על (weep over, dead-focused
     idiom) in 11:37-38 vs. תנה ל in 11:40 (recount to/for).
KEY: מִיָּמִים יָמִימָה — "from days to days" distributive idiom = "yearly"
     (cf. Exo 13:10; 1 Sam 1:3; 1 Sam 2:19 — all CULTIC/PILGRIMAGE contexts)

וַיִּדַּר יִשְׂרָאֵל נֶדֶר לַיהוָה וַיֹּאמַר אִם־נָתֹן תִּתֵּן אֶת־הָעָם הַזֶּה בְּיָדִי וְהַחֲרַמְתִּי אֶת־עָרֵיהֶם

=== 1 Samuel 1:11 — Hannah's vow (controlling parallel) ===
וַתִּדֹּר נֶדֶר וַתֹּאמַר יְהוָה צְבָאוֹת אִם־רָאֹה תִרְאֶה בָּעֳנִי אֲמָתֶךָ וּזְכַרְתַּנִי וְלֹא־תִשְׁכַּח אֶת־אֲמָתֶךָ וְנָתַתָּה לַאֲמָתְךָ זֶרַע אֲנָשִׁים וּנְתַתִּיו לַיהוָה כָּל־יְמֵי חַיָּיו וּמוֹרָה לֹא־יַעֲלֶה עַל־רֹאשׁוֹ

Apodosis: וּנְתַתִּיו לַיהוָה כָּל־יְמֵי חַיָּיו
          ו+Qal.Perf.1s+3ms נתן + ל + YHWH + "ALL THE DAYS OF HIS LIFE"

CRITICAL LEXICAL CONTRAST vs JEPHTHAH:
- Hannah:   וּנְתַתִּיו לַיהוָה  (natan = "I will give/assign him")
           + כָּל־יְמֵי חַיָּיו  ("all the days of his life" = LIFELONG)
           + Nazarite-separation marker ("no razor upon his head")
- Jephthah: וְהָיָה לַיהוָה       (hayah = "shall be to YHWH" — plain existence)
           + וְהַעֲלִיתִהוּ עֹלָה  ("and I will offer him up [as] burnt-offering")
           + NO life-duration clause
           + NO separation/consecration marker

The two vows employ DIFFERENT lexical registers:
- Hannah's lexicon = NATAN + LIFELONG DURATION = dedication/lifelong service
  (cf. Levites wholly "given" natan-ed in Num 3:9; 8:16; 8:19)
- Jephthah's lexicon = HAYAH LA-YHWH + Hiphil ALAH + OLAH-noun = SACRIFICIAL
  cultic collocation (cf. Gen 8:20; 22:2, 13; Lev 17:8 — Hiphil alah +
  olah is the technical burnt-offering phrase)

If Jephthah had intended Hannah-style lifelong-dedication, the available
lexicon was natan + kol yemei chayyav. He did not use it. His chosen phrasing
tracks the sacrificial register, not the dedication register.

=== Genesis 22:2 — Akedah ===

=== 2 Kings 3:27 — Mesha sacrifice CONTRAST ===
וַיִּקַּח אֶת־בְּנוֹ הַבְּכוֹר אֲשֶׁר־יִמְלֹךְ תַּחְתָּיו וַיַּעֲלֵהוּ עֹלָה עַל־הַחֹמָה

KEY: וַיַּעֲלֵהוּ עֹלָה (Verb.Hiphil.Wayq.3ms+3ms עלה + עלה "burnt-offering")
     "AND HE OFFERED HIM UP [AS] BURNT-OFFERING"

Diagnostic contrast with Jdg 11:39: when the OT narrator reports an actual
case of human immolation in burnt-offering mode, the Hiphil עלה + עולה
collocation IS USED. Jdg 11:39 has plain עשׂה (wayya'as) — no sacrificial
vocabulary. The narrator's lexical restraint at Jdg 11:39 is conspicuous.

=== Jeremiah 7:31 — Divine Disavowal ===
וּבָנוּ בָּמוֹת הַתֹּפֶת אֲשֶׁר בְּגֵיא בֶן־הִנֹּם לִשְׂרֹף אֶת־בְּנֵיהֶם וְאֶת־בְּנֹתֵיהֶם בָּאֵשׁ אֲשֶׁר לֹא צִוִּיתִי וְלֹא עָלְתָה עַל־לִבִּי

KEY: לִשְׂרֹף אֶת־בְּנֵיהֶם וְאֶת־בְּנֹתֵיהֶם — "to burn their sons and daughters
     in the fire" (verb שׂרף "burn" used — sacrificial lexicon)
KEY: אֲשֶׁר לֹא צִוִּיתִי (Piel.Perf.1s צוה "I did not command")
KEY: וְלֹא עָלְתָה עַל־לִבִּי (Qal.Perf.3fs עלה) "neither came it up into my
     heart" — YHWH's express disavowal of human sacrifice as something He
     ever commanded or even conceived.

This forecloses ANY reading in which YHWH accepted/commanded Jephthah's daughter
as a literal burnt offering. Regardless of whether Jephthah's vow was understood
by himself as literal sacrifice, YHWH's verdict on such offerings in Jer 7:31 is
unambiguous: not commanded, not conceived, not accepted.

=== Genesis 24:16 — Rebekah lo yada ish ===
וְהַנַּעֲרָ טֹבַת מַרְאֶה מְאֹד בְּתוּלָה וְאִישׁ לֹא יְדָעָהּ

KEY: בְּתוּלָה (Noun.fs.Abs) "a virgin" + וְאִישׁ לֹא יְדָעָהּ
     (WX-QATAL: ו + אִישׁ + Qal.Perf.3ms+3fs) "and a man had not known her"
Rebekah is ALIVE; the lo-yada-ish formula describes her living sexual-
status. Strongest parallel to Jdg 11:39 WX-QATAL.

=== 1 Kings 1:4 — Abishag lo yada ===
וְהַנַּעֲרָה יָפָה עַד־מְאֹד וַתְּהִי לַמֶּלֶךְ סֹכֶנֶת וַתְּשָׁרְתֵהוּ וְהַמֶּלֶךְ לֹא יְדָעָהּ

KEY: וְהַמֶּלֶךְ לֹא יְדָעָהּ — WX-QATAL of virginal attachment (Abishag
     ALIVE and serving David throughout his remaining lifetime). Structural
     parallel to Jdg 11:39 — BUT note: Abishag lives on and serves (vv.
     continuing into 1 Kings 2). The construction describes ONGOING living
     state, not a death-event aftermath.

=== Leviticus 27:2 — Vow-Valuation ===
דַּבֵּר אֶל־בְּנֵי יִשְׂרָאֵל וְאָמַרְתָּ אֲלֵהֶם אִישׁ כִּי יַפְלִא נֶדֶר בְּעֶרְכְּךָ נְפָשֹׁת לַיהוָה

KEY: כִּי יַפְלִא נֶדֶר — "when [a man] makes a singular/special vow"
     Verb H6381 פלא (Hiphil "make special/extraordinary") + cognate accusative נֶדֶר
KEY: בְּעֶרְכְּךָ נְפָשֹׁת לַיהוָה — "by thy ערך (valuation) persons [shall be] to YHWH"
     H6187 ערך is the priest-assigned monetary valuation; Lev 27:3-8 details
     the age/sex-graded tariff (female 5-20 yr = 10 shekels; 20-60 yr = 30
     shekels). This is Torah's built-in off-ramp for person-vows.

=== Leviticus 27:28 — Cherem Law ===
אַךְ כָּל־חֵרֶם אֲשֶׁר יַחֲרִם אִישׁ לַיהוָה מִכָּל־אֲשֶׁר לוֹ מֵאָדָם וּבְהֵמָה וּמִשְּׂדֵה אֲחֻזָּתוֹ לֹא יִמָּכֵר וְלֹא יִגָּאֵל

KEY: כָּל־חֵרֶם אֲשֶׁר יַחֲרִם — CHEREM (H2764 noun + Hiphil H2763 verb)
     - IS NOT a regular neder
     - IS NOT redeemable (לֹא יִגָּאֵל)
     - Belongs to the category of judicial-ban (Josh 7 Achan; 1 Sam 15 Amalek)

=== Leviticus 27:29 — Cherem of Persons ===
כָּל־חֵרֶם אֲשֶׁר יָחֳרַם מִן־הָאָדָם לֹא יִפָּדֶה מוֹת יוּמָת

KEY: לֹא יִפָּדֶה — "shall not be redeemed" (Niphal H6299 פדה)
KEY: מוֹת יוּמָת — "shall surely be put to death" (tautological inf.abs. +
     Hophal H4191 מות)

IMPORTANT: Lev 27:28-29 is CHEREM (judicial ban), NOT ordinary NEDER.
Jephthah vowed a נֶדֶר, not a חֵרֶם. The non-redeemable-death-penalty
provision of Lev 27:28-29 does NOT apply to private votive pledges;
it applies to public judicial ban on Molech-worshipers (Lev 20:2-5),
apostate cities (Deut 13:15), and Amalek/Canaan-type conquest-devotions.
A private person-vow (neder) falls under Lev 27:1-8 — ALWAYS redeemable
via the priest-administered ערך (valuation). The Targum Jonathan gloss on
Jdg 11:39 (via Clarke 28231) takes exactly this position: Jephthah's fault
lay in NOT CONSULTING PHINEHAS, who would have applied Lev 27:1-8
redemption.

=== Numbers 30:2 — Vow-Payment Absolutism + Num 30:3-5 Father's Annulment ===
Num 30:2: If a man vow a vow unto the LORD... he shall not break his word,
          he shall do according to all that proceedeth out of his mouth.
Num 30:3-5: A woman's vow, made "in her father's house in her youth," is
            SUBJECT to her father's annulment on the day he hears it. If
            he disallows it, "the LORD shall forgive her, because her
            father disallowed her" (v. 5).

NOTE: This provides a THIRD off-ramp Jephthah could have invoked AS FATHER.
Yet the narrator records NO annulment and NO priest-consultation. Num 30
applies to the class of vow (woman in father's house) of which the daughter
is subject — BUT Jephthah is the VOWER, not the daughter. His own oath
falls under Num 30:2 (a man's vow) which he interprets as irrevocable.

=== Deuteronomy 12:31 — Categorical Prohibition ===
לֹא־תַעֲשֶׂה כֵן לַיהוָה אֱלֹהֶיךָ כִּי כָּל־תּוֹעֲבַת יְהוָה אֲשֶׁר שָׂנֵא עָשׂוּ לֵאלֹהֵיהֶם כִּי גַם אֶת־בְּנֵיהֶם וְאֶת־בְּנֹתֵיהֶם יִשְׂרְפוּ בָאֵשׁ לֵאלֹהֵיהֶם

KEY: לֹא־תַעֲשֶׂה כֵן לַיהוָה — "THOU SHALT NOT DO SO UNTO YHWH"
     Explicit prohibition against applying Canaanite-style child immolation
     to YHWH-worship. Jephthah's act, on the literal reading, would be
     this exact prohibited act.

=== Deuteronomy 18:10 — Pass-Through-Fire Prohibition ===
לֹא־יִמָּצֵא בְךָ מַעֲבִיר בְּנוֹ־וּבִתּוֹ בָּאֵשׁ

KEY: מַעֲבִיר (Hiphil.Ptcp.ms עבר) בְּנוֹ־וּבִתּוֹ בָּאֵשׁ
     "one causing his son or his daughter to pass through the fire"
     Categorical prohibition, no exception for private vow-motivated use.
