=== JEPHTHAH ===
(A judge (leader, hero) of Israel)
- Illegitimate, and therefore not entitled to inherit his father's property
  JDG 11:1, 2
- Escapes the violence of his half-brothers; lives in the land of Tob
  JDG 11:3
- Recalled from the land of Tob by the elders of Gilead JDG 11:5
- Made captain of the army JDG 11:5-11
- Made head of the land of Gilead JDG 11:7-11
- His message to the king of the Ammonites JDG 11:12-28
- Leads the army of Israel against the Ammonites JDG 11:29-33
- His rash vow concerning his daughter JDG 11:31, 34-40
- Falsely accused by the Ephraimites JDG 12:1
- Leads the army of the Gileadites against the Ephraimites JDG 12:4
- Leads Israel for six years, dies, and is buried in Gilead JDG 12:7
- Faith of HEB 11:32

NOTE: Nave's entry classifies 11:31, 34-40 explicitly under "rash vow" —
an interpretive editorial choice reflecting the literal-sacrifice reading.
This is Nave's interpretation, not Scripture's verdict.

=== MOLECH ===
- Also called MOLOCH and MILCOM
- An idol of the Ammonites ACT 7:43
- Worshiped by the wives of Solomon, and by Solomon 1KI 11:1-8
- Children sacrificed to: 2KI 23:10; JER 32:35; 2KI 16:3; 21:6;
  2CH 28:3; ISA 57:5; JER 7:31; EZK 16:20, 21; 20:26, 31; 23:37, 39
- See LEV 18:21; 20:2-5

NOTE: Molech was the idol of AMMON specifically (Act 7:43; 1 Kgs 11:5
"Milcom the abomination of the Ammonites"). Jephthah was fighting Ammon
(Jdg 11). If Jephthah had offered his daughter as a burnt offering, he
would have been replicating the very abomination of the god of the enemy
he had just defeated — a grotesque inversion that exacerbates the
theological problem.

CROSS-THEMATIC SYNTHESIS (for Focus Areas)
================================================================

Focus Area 1-3 (Grammar of vow) — Nave's JEPHTHAH + VOWS entries
supply the verse list (Jdg 11:1-40; 12:1-7; Heb 11:32 for the man;
Lev 27, Num 30, Deut 12, Ecc 5 for the legal framework; Gen 28:20,
Num 21:2, 1 Sam 1:11, 2 Sam 15:7 for the vow-parallels).

Focus Area 4-5 (bethulim, tanah) — Nave's VIRGIN entry places
"Virginity of, bewailed JDG 11:37-39" in the living-women's
virginity category alongside Deut 22:13-21, Lev 21:14, Lam 1:4;
2:10 (virgins mourning in temple) — all LIVING-status references.

Focus Area 6 (neder vs cherem, redemption) — Nave's DEDICATION entry
distinguishes Lev 27:1-25 redemption-mechanisms from Lev 27:28-29
cherem non-redemption. The vow-of-persons regulation (Lev 27:1-8)
is under DEDICATION, not under cherem. Lev 27:1-13 places Jephthah's
daughter-vow in the redeemable category by default.

Focus Area 7 (human-sacrifice prohibition) — Nave's MOLECH entry
gathers the full prohibition-and-condemnation corpus: Lev 18:21;
20:2-5; 2 Kgs 23:10; Jer 32:35; 7:31; 2 Kgs 16:3; 21:6; Isa 57:5;
Ezk 16:20, 21; 20:26, 31; 23:37, 39. The act Jephthah's literal
reading would commit is explicitly the act of Molech worship that
the Ammonites (his enemy) practiced.

Focus Area 8 (Hannah parallel) — Nave's NAZARITE + VOWS (Instances)
+ DEDICATION entries all cross-reference 1 Sam 1:11 as the structural
dedication parallel.

Focus Area 9 (Spirit + Judges) — Nave's JEPHTHAH entry under "Leads the
army of Israel against the Ammonites JDG 11:29-33" scopes the Spirit-
inhabitation to the military campaign, not to the vow episode.

Focus Area 10 (Heb 11:32) — Nave's JEPHTHAH entry closes with "Faith of
HEB 11:32" — ONE line. The faith-commendation is unadorned and does
not specify vow or victory.
