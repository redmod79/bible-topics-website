opposite of what a literal Jdg 11:39 would require.

=== Lev 27:2 ===
Concepts found: LIFE (nefesh H5315 — "the persons")

Same-book matches: only Lev 11 food-law verses tagged under LIFE
(semantic-classifier gap — the priestly vow-law did not match other
priestly contexts on concepts).

Same-author matches (Moses): Deut 10:12, 10:22, 11:13, 11:18 — all
"heart and soul" covenant fidelity texts. The semantic-classifier
associates the vow-of-persons regulation with whole-life devotion,
not sacrificial death.

Other books: 1 Chr 11:19 — David's rejection of blood-of-men offering
("shall I drink the blood of these men that have put their lives in
jeopardy?") — STRONG conceptual parallel: David recoils from symbolic-
drinking of life-endangered men's sacrifice. Also 1 Chr 22:19 — "set
your heart and soul to seek the LORD" + 1 Chr 28:9 + 2 Chr 17:16
(Zichri's self-consecration). The Chronicler's theology = person-
dedication is SOUL-SERVICE, not death-offering.

================================================================
KEY SEMANTIC-CLASSIFIER OBSERVATION
================================================================

The concept_context.py classifier independently tagged:
- Jdg 11:31 (vow) with SACRIFICE (because of olah)
- Jdg 11:39 (fulfillment) with FULFILLMENT (neutral asah) — NOT SACRIFICE
- 1 Sam 1:11 (Hannah) with LIFE (because of chay)
- Lev 27:2 with LIFE (because of nefesh)

That the fulfillment-verse (Jdg 11:39) sheds the SACRIFICE tag that the
vow-verse (Jdg 11:31) carries is grammatically and lexically significant.
The narrator's verb-choice (עשׂה asah vs. עלה Hiphil + olah) registers on
automatic semantic classifiers.
