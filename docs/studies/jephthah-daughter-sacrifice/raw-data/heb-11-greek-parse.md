ALL nine aorist-verbs in vv. 33-34 belong to the military-deliverance
semantic field — armies defeated, promises received, mouths of lions shut,
fire quenched, sword escaped, weakness strengthened, battle-valor gained,
enemy camps turned.

NONE of the nine aorists describes a priestly/cultic act, a vow, a sacrifice,
or any action specifically marriageable to Jdg 11:30-31 or 11:39 (the vow or
its fulfillment). Every aorist maps naturally to Jdg 11:29 + 11:32-33 —
Jephthah's SPIRIT-empowered military campaign against Ammon, 20 cities smitten,
Ammon subdued.

CONTROLS (same principle elsewhere in Heb 11):
- Samson — κατηγωνίσαντο + ἐδυναμώθησαν cover his lion, jawbone, and temple-
  pull-down; Heb 11 does NOT endorse his Philistine wife (Jdg 14:1-3) or
  his visit to the harlot (Jdg 16:1).
- David — ἐργάσαντο δικαιοσύνην; Heb 11 does NOT endorse his Bathsheba/Uriah
  affair.
- Gideon — κατηγωνίσαντο; Heb 11 does NOT endorse his gold-ephod idolatry
  (Jdg 8:27).
- Barak — ἐπέτυχον ἐπαγγελιῶν (received the promise Deborah spoke).

THE FAITH-ROLL COMMENDS DISCRETE FAITH-ACTS, NOT WHOLE BIOGRAPHIES. Heb 11:32
is therefore SYMMETRICAL on the Jephthah-vow question — it commends his
faith in the Ammonite campaign (ch. 11:29-33) without saying anything one
way or the other about the vow. The vow is not among the nine aorists; the
military deliverance is.
