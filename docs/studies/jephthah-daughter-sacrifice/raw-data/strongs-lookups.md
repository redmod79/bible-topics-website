H3318 yatsa hayyotze participle referent-scope (Jdg 11:31):
- Out of 51 Qal ms absolute participle forms in OT, approx distribution:
  - Human individual (~14): named person exits a place
  - Human collective (~20): military/civic "going out"
  - Offspring from loins (4): Gen 15:4, Gen 17:6, 1 Ki 8:19, 2 Chr 6:9
  - YHWH/divine agent (~5): Isa 26:21 etc.
  - Inanimate/natural (~15): rivers, lightning, word-from-mouth
  - Animal subject: ZERO direct cases
- Specifically, "hayyotze + lo + lo-qeratiti" / "coming out to meet me"
  + "doors of my house" is UNIQUE to Jdg 11:31 in the OT. Closest
  idiomatic neighbors (Num 27:17 "goeth out and cometh in"; 1 Ki 3:7;
  Prov 7:15) invariably describe HUMAN leadership/social movement.
- The formulaic frame SKEWS strongly toward a HUMAN referent (per the
  library entry): "The grammatical pattern yotze + liqrati ('coming out
  to meet me') is itself strongly human — meeting (qara / liqrat) is a
  social act performed by persons, not animals, across its ~100 OT
  occurrences."
- COUNTER-POINT (from the Josephus paraphrase in Ant. 5.7.10): "what
  living creature soever should first meet him" — Josephus takes the
  participle as GENERIC creature, not specifically human. The Hebrew
  pattern + Gileadite house-courtyard setting (livestock sometimes kept
  inside family compounds) could admit a wider referent, but the
  GRAMMATICAL DEFAULT is human.

H3173 yachiyd (Jdg 11:34):
- 12 OT occurrences — LXX splits as ἀγαπητός (7x, in sacrifice/mourning
  frames — Gen 22:2, 12, 16 Isaac; Jdg 11:34; Jer 6:26; Amos 8:10;
  Zech 12:10) and μονογενής (4x, in the Psalmist's "one irreplaceable
  life" frame).
- Jdg 11:34 is the ONLY OT verse where LXX uses BOTH ἀγαπητή AND μονογενής
  (paired — "only [and] beloved") — importing the full Akedah-echo
  vocabulary into the Jephthah narrative.
- 42% of yachid occurrences cluster in "only-child-threatened-with-
  sacrifice" contexts: Gen 22 (Isaac averted), Jdg 11 (Jephthah's daughter
  — contested), Zech 12:10 (Messianic pierced one — arrested by resurrection).

H6187 'erek (Lev 27:2-8 redemption valuation):
- 35 OT tokens in 29 verses; 20 of those in Leviticus (Lev 27 redemption
  tariff + Lev 5 trespass-offering).
- The canonical off-ramp: "Lev 27:1-8 establishes the canonical Torah
  mechanism for discharging a vow that has devoted a person to YHWH:
  (1) the vow is binding, (2) but the person is not to be literally
  offered; instead the priest assigns a silver valuation, (3) the poverty
  clause scales the tariff, (4) only objects placed under cherem are
  non-redeemable. The category EXPLICITLY CONTEMPLATES THE SCENARIO OF
  JDG 11 — a hastily-sworn person-vow. Torah's answer is: pay the
  priestly valuation. The silver discharges the vow; the person goes home."

H5414 natan vs H5927 Hiphil 'alah (vow-verb contrast):
- natan laYHWH + kol yemei chayyav = Hannah/Levite LIVING-SERVICE dedication
- Hiphil 'alah + 'olah (burnt offering) = technical cultic immolation
- Jdg 11:31 uses HAYAH laYHWH (plain) + Hiphil 'alah + 'olah (technical
  sacrificial collocation). Neither Hannah-pattern (natan) nor Lev 27:2
  person-vow-pattern (yaphli neder + 'erek) is lexically present.

H1331 bethulim (abstract virginity-state):
- 10 OT tokens in 9 verses. Every occurrence describes a LIVING woman's
  sexual status (Lev 21:13 priestly marriage; Deut 22:14-20 bride-defamation
  trial; Ezek 23:3, 8 figurative of Israel's youth; Jdg 11:37-38 Jephthah's
  daughter) — NONE describes a deceased woman. The abstract plural signals
  "state of virginity as a CONDITION," the condition Jephthah's daughter
  mourns for TWO MONTHS.

H8567 tanah (dis legomenon):
- Only Jdg 5:11 (Piel impf. "they shall recount") and Jdg 11:40 (Piel inf.
  const.) — confined to Judges.
- LXX splits: Jdg 5:11 → διηγέομαι (recount); Jdg 11:40 → θρηνεῖν (lament).
- The 5:11 control is CELEBRATIVE — "they shall rehearse the righteous
  acts of the LORD." If Jdg 5:11 governs Jdg 11:40, the annual rite is
  celebrative commemoration, not funeral lament.
- Syntactic frame in 11:40: ל (of interest) + bat-Yiftach, NOT עַל (of
  object-of-mourning). One mourns עַל someone; one recounts ל someone.

H2076 zabach:
- 127 OT verses, 149 tokens; the standard sacrifice-slaughter verb.
- CONSPICUOUS ABSENCE from Jdg 11:39. The narrator does not say "wayyizbahch
  et bitto" ("and he sacrificed his daughter"). Instead: wayya'as (plain
  "do/make") — a lexical restraint that cannot be read as the equivalent
  of a sacrifice-verb elsewhere.

H2763 charam / H2764 cherem:
- The PUBLIC JUDICIAL BAN category (Achan, Amalek, Arad-cities).
- Lev 27:28-29 explicitly restricts non-redemption + death-penalty to
  cherem cases. Lev 27:1-8 covers ordinary neder — ALL redeemable.
- Num 21:2 Israel's vow uses charam (not olah); it is a LAWFUL public
  judicial ban. NOT a precedent for private person-immolation.
- Jephthah's vow is נֶדֶר (Jdg 11:30: "vayyiddar Yiphtach NEDER la-YHWH"),
  NOT חֵרֶם. So Lev 27:28-29 does not apply; Lev 27:1-8 does.
