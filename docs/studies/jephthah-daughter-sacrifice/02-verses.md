### Judges 11:29-31 (Spirit + Vow — CENTRAL)
> **Jdg 11:29** Then the Spirit of the LORD came upon Jephthah, and he passed over Gilead, and Manasseh, and passed over Mizpeh of Gilead, and from Mizpeh of Gilead he passed over [unto] the children of Ammon.
> **Jdg 11:30** And Jephthah vowed a vow unto the LORD, and said, If thou shalt without fail deliver the children of Ammon into mine hands,
> **Jdg 11:31** Then it shall be, that whatsoever cometh forth of the doors of my house to meet me, when I return in peace from the children of Ammon, shall surely be the LORD'S, and I will offer it up for a burnt offering.

**Hebrew parsing of v. 30 (key elements):**
- וַיִּדַּר (Qal.Wayq.3ms נדר) "and he vowed" + cognate accusative נֶדֶר — solemn vow-formula
- אִם (Conj "if") + נָתוֹן תִּתֵּן (inf.abs.+Impf.2ms נתן) — emphatic protasis "if thou wilt indeed deliver"

**Hebrew parsing of v. 31 (CENTRAL):**
- וְהָיָה (Qal.Perf.3ms weqatal) — apodosis head
- הַיּוֹצֵא אֲשֶׁר יֵצֵא (Qal.Ptcp.ms + relative + Impf.3ms יצא) — *casus pendens* + cognate-verb relative; maximally indefinite referent
- לִקְרָאתִי (לקרא Qal.InfCon+1s) "to meet me" — social-encounter idiom skewing human
- וְהָיָה לַיהוָה (weqatal + dative) — "and shall be to YHWH" (plain dative; NOT Hannah's natan+kol-yemei-chayyav)
- וְהַעֲלִיתִהוּ עֹלָה (Verb.Hiphil.Perf.1s+3ms עלה "cause to go up" + Noun.fs.Abs עולה "burnt-offering") — technical cultic collocation (Hiphil alah + olah) used at Gen 8:20; 22:2, 13; Lev 17:8; 2 Kgs 3:27

**Grammar-reference entries:**
- `grammar-reference/passages/jdg-11-30-31.md` — full analysis
- `grammar-reference/hebrew/syntax-conditional-vow.md` — im + impf. protasis / weqatal apodosis template
- `grammar-reference/hebrew/syntax-waw-conjunction.md` — waw conjunctive/disjunctive debate
- `grammar-reference/hebrew/weqatal-apodosis-vow.md` — weqatal chain in vow apodoses

**The disjunctive-waw question (v. 31):** The third weqatal וְהַעֲלִיתִהוּ *can* grammatically be read disjunctively ("OR I will offer it up") per W-O'C §39.2 polyfunctionality of waw + legal-enumeration parallels (Exo 21:15 — "his father OR his mother"). But default force of a weqatal chain is conjunctive ("and"). Hebrew has a dedicated disjunctive particle אוֹ (used in v. 34 "neither son אוֹ or daughter"); v. 31 does NOT use אוֹ. LXX A-text uses καί (conjunctive); B-text uses asyndeton. Neither LXX recension uses ἤ. Grammar admits the disjunctive reading as MARKED; the unmarked default is conjunctive.

### Judges 11:34 (Daughter Emerges — yechidah Akedah Echo)
> **Jdg 11:34** And Jephthah came to Mizpeh unto his house, and, behold, his daughter came out to meet him with timbrels and with dances: and she [was his] only child; beside her he had neither son nor daughter.

**Hebrew parsing (central):**
- יֹצֵאת (Qal.Ptcp.fs.Abs יצא) "going out" — feminine counterpart to v. 31 הַיּוֹצֵא
- לִקְרָאתוֹ (לקרא Qal.InfCon+3ms) "to meet him" — social-encounter idiom
- בְתֻפִּים וּבִמְחֹלוֹת — "with timbrels and dances" (victory-welcome cultural marker)
- וְרַק הִיא יְחִידָה — "and only she [was] an only-one" (Adj.fs.Abs H3173 יָחִיד) — direct lexical echo of Gen 22:2 אֶת־בִּנְךָ אֶת־יְחִידְךָ ("thy son, thine only son")
- אֵין לוֹ מִמֶּנּוּ בֵּן אוֹ־בַת — "he had from him neither son OR daughter" — *note the explicit use of disjunctive particle אוֹ here* (contrast with v. 31 where the waw, not אוֹ, was used; the author plainly had אוֹ in his idiolect but did not reach for it in v. 31)

### Judges 11:35 (Jephthah's Lament)
> **Jdg 11:35** And it came to pass, when he saw her, that he rent his clothes, and said, Alas, my daughter! thou hast brought me very low, and thou art one of them that trouble me: for I have opened my mouth unto the LORD, and I cannot go back.

**Hebrew parsing:**
- הַכְרֵעַ הִכְרַעְתִּנִי (Hiphil.Inf.Abs + Hiphil.Perf.2fs+1s כרע) — tautological inf.abs. "thou hast utterly bowed me down"
- פָּצִיתִי פִי אֶל־יְהוָה (Qal.Perf.1s פצה + pi + prep.+YHWH) — "I have opened my mouth unto YHWH"
- וְלֹא אוּכַל לָשׁוּב (we + Neg + Qal.Impf.1s יכל + Qal.InfCon שוב) — "and I cannot go/turn back"

**Observation:** Jephthah's own interpretation ("I cannot go back") reflects his subjective sense of vow-irrevocability. But Torah explicitly provides off-ramps he does not invoke: Lev 5:4-5 (rash-oath guilt-offering); Lev 27:1-8 (neder redemption by priestly valuation); Num 30 (father's annulment of daughter's vow — inapplicable since Jephthah is vower, not daughter; but Num 30:2 governs the man's own vow-obligation, which the Lev 5:4-5 pathway can still mitigate). "I cannot go back" = Jephthah's subjective conviction, not Torah's legal assessment.

### Judges 11:36 (Daughter's Consent)
> **Jdg 11:36** And she said unto him, My father, [if] thou hast opened thy mouth unto the LORD, do to me according to that which hath proceeded out of thy mouth; forasmuch as the LORD hath taken vengeance for thee of thine enemies, [even] of the children of Ammon.

**Hebrew parsing:**
- עֲשֵׂה לִי (Qal.Impv.2ms עשׂה + prep.+1s) "do to me" — SAME verb (עשׂה) as 11:39 climax; neutral "do/make" not sacrificial
- כַּאֲשֶׁר יָצָא מִפִּיךָ (per. + Qal.Perf.3ms יצא + prep.+2ms) "according as it went out from thy mouth" — Num 30:2 legal idiom ("all that proceedeth out of his mouth")

### Judges 11:37-38 (Daughter's Two-Month Mountain Mourning of bethulim)
> **Jdg 11:37** And she said unto her father, Let this thing be done for me: let me alone two months, that I may go up and down upon the mountains, and bewail my virginity, I and my fellows.
> **Jdg 11:38** And he said, Go. And he sent her away [for] two months: and she went with her companions, and bewailed her virginity upon the mountains.

**Hebrew parsing:**
- יֵעָשֶׂה (Verb.Niphal.Impf.3ms עשׂה) — "let it be done" (polite passive — daughter requesting, not commanding)
- הַרְפֵּה מִמֶּנִּי (Hiphil.Impv.2ms רפה + prep+1s) — "let me alone / desist from me"
- וְאֵלְכָה (Qal.Impf.1s cohortative) — "let me go"
- וְיָרַדְתִּי (weqatal Qal.Perf.1s) — "and I will descend"
- וְאֶבְכֶּה (weqatal Qal.Impf.1s) — "and I will weep"
- **עַל־בְּתוּלַי** (prep+Noun.fp.Abs+1s בתולים H1331) — "for my virginity-state"
- Repetition in v. 38: וַתֵּבְךְּ עַל־בְּתוּלֶיהָ (Qal.Wayq.3fs + bethulim+3fs) — "and she wept over her virginity" — narrator echoes daughter's own phrasing

**Library reference:** `word-studies/H1331-bthuwliym.md` — abstract plural of condition; ALL 10 OT occurrences describe LIVING women's virginity-state, never a deceased woman's past status.

### Judges 11:39 (Fulfillment — THE CENTRAL VERSE)
> **Jdg 11:39** And it came to pass at the end of two months, that she returned unto her father, who did with her [according] to his vow which he had vowed: and she knew no man. And it was a custom in Israel,

**Hebrew parsing:**
- וַיַּעַשׂ לָהּ אֶת־נִדְרוֹ (Qal.Wayq.3ms עשׂה + prep+3fs + direct-obj + noun+3ms) — "AND HE DID to her his vow"
  - ⚠ CRITICAL: Narrator uses **עשׂה** (plain "do/make"), NOT:
    - **זבח** (H2076 zabach — slaughter/sacrifice) — ABSENT
    - **שׁחט** (H7819 shachat — technical slaughter) — ABSENT
    - **שׂרף** (H8313 saraph — burn) — ABSENT
    - **עלה Hiphil + עולה** (Gen 8:20; 22:2, 13; 2 Kgs 3:27 Mesha's identical act) — ABSENT
    - **הֶעֱבִיר בָּאֵשׁ** (pass through fire; 2 Kgs 16:3; 21:6; Jer 32:35) — ABSENT
- אֲשֶׁר נָדָר (relative + Qal.Perf.3ms נדר) — cognate-accusative "which he had vowed" (figura etymologica)
- **וְהִיא לֹא־יָדְעָה אִישׁ** — **WX-QATAL** construction: waw + fronted PersPron.3fs הִיא + Neg + Qal.Perf.3fs ידע + Noun אִישׁ
  - Library reference: `grammar-reference/hebrew/syntax-wx-qatal-circumstantial.md`
  - Construction type = circumstantial / disjunctive / stative-resultant (W-O'C §39.2.3; GKC §156)
  - BREAKS the wayyiqtol chain between וַיַּעַשׂ and וַתְּהִי
  - Pragmatic force: parenthetical background-state notice about her sexual/marital status
  - The narrator's chosen interpretive gloss on "doing the vow" is in SEXUAL/MARITAL terms, not mortality terms
- וַתְּהִי־חֹק בְּיִשְׂרָאֵל — "and it became a statute in Israel"

### Judges 11:40 (Annual Four-Day Rite)
> **Jdg 11:40** [That] the daughters of Israel went yearly to lament the daughter of Jephthah the Gileadite four days in a year.

**Hebrew parsing:**
- מִיָּמִים יָמִימָה (prep+yom+yom-locative) — distributive "from days to days" = "yearly" (Exo 13:10; 1 Sam 1:3; 2:19 — all cultic/pilgrimage)
- תֵּלַכְנָה (Qal.Impf.3fp הלך) — "they would go" (iterative habitual)
- בְּנוֹת יִשְׂרָאֵל — "daughters of Israel"
- **לְתַנּוֹת** (prep+Verb.Piel.InfCon **תנה** H8567) — dis legomenon; the ONLY other verbal OT occurrence is Jdg 5:11 יְתַנּוּ "they rehearse/recount the righteous acts of the LORD" — CELEBRATIVE. LXX splits: 5:11 → διηγέομαι "recount"; 11:40 → θρηνεῖν "lament."
- לְבַת־יִפְתָּח (prep + construct) — ל of interest/benefit (one recounts TO/FOR), not עַל of object-of-mourning (one weeps OVER)
- אַרְבַּעַת יָמִים בַּשָּׁנָה — "four days in the year"

**Library reference:** `word-studies/H8567-tanah.md` — dis legomenon; ritual recounting, celebrative in Jdg 5:11.

## C. Vow-Redemption Law — Leviticus 27 (FULL CHAPTER)

> **Lev 27:1** And the LORD spake unto Moses, saying,
> **Lev 27:2** Speak unto the children of Israel, and say unto them, When a man shall make a singular vow, the persons [shall be] for the LORD by thy estimation.
> **Lev 27:3** And thy estimation shall be of the male from twenty years old even unto sixty years old, even thy estimation shall be fifty shekels of silver, after the shekel of the sanctuary.
> **Lev 27:4** And if it [be] a female, then thy estimation shall be thirty shekels.
> **Lev 27:5** And if [it be] from five years old even unto twenty years old, then thy estimation shall be of the male twenty shekels, and for the female ten shekels.
> **Lev 27:6** And if [it be] from a month old even unto five years old, then thy estimation shall be of the male five shekels of silver, and for the female thy estimation [shall be] three shekels of silver.
> **Lev 27:7** And if [it be] from sixty years old and above; if [it be] a male, then thy estimation shall be fifteen shekels, and for the female ten shekels.
> **Lev 27:8** But if he be poorer than thy estimation, then he shall present himself before the priest, and the priest shall value him; according to his ability that vowed shall the priest value him.
> [vv. 9-25: animals, houses, fields — redemption tariff; + sanctuary-shekel = 20 gerahs]
> **Lev 27:26** Only the firstling of the beasts, which should be the LORD'S firstling, no man shall sanctify it; whether [it be] ox, or sheep: it [is] the LORD'S.
> **Lev 27:27** And if [it be] of an unclean beast, then he shall redeem [it] according to thine estimation, and shall add a fifth [part] of it thereto: or if it be not redeemed, then it shall be sold according to thy estimation.
> **Lev 27:28** Notwithstanding no devoted thing, that a man shall devote unto the LORD of all that he hath, [both] of man and beast, and of the field of his possession, shall be sold or redeemed: every devoted thing [is] most holy unto the LORD.
> **Lev 27:29** None devoted, which shall be devoted of men, shall be redeemed; [but] shall surely be put to death.
> **Lev 27:30-34** [Tithe laws; closing formula]

**Hebrew parsing of Lev 27:2 (CENTRAL):**
- כִּי יַפְלִא נֶדֶר (conj + Hiphil.Impf.3ms פלא H6381 + Noun נֶדֶר H5088) — "when [a man] makes a special/singular vow"
- **בְּעֶרְכְּךָ נְפָשֹׁת לַיהוָה** — "by thy valuation [the] persons [are] to/for YHWH"
- H6187 עֶרֶךְ is the priestly monetary valuation (library: `H6187-erek.md`) — redemption by silver, age- and sex-scaled tariff

**Hebrew parsing of Lev 27:28-29 (CHEREM — distinct category):**
- חֵרֶם אֲשֶׁר יַחֲרִם אִישׁ (Noun.ms.Abs חרם + relative + Hiphil.Impf.3ms חרם) — "ban/devoted-thing which a man shall devote"
- לֹא יִמָּכֵר וְלֹא יִגָּאֵל (Neg + Niphal.Impf.3ms מכר + Neg + Niphal.Impf.3ms גאל) — "shall not be sold AND shall not be redeemed" — non-redemption is explicit
- v. 29: כָּל־חֵרֶם אֲשֶׁר יָחֳרַם מִן־הָאָדָם לֹא יִפָּדֶה מוֹת יוּמָת — "every cherem which shall be devoted of men, shall not be redeemed; he shall surely be put to death."

**CRITICAL LEGAL POINT:** Lev 27:28-29 governs CHEREM (H2764 judicial ban — the Achan/Amalek category; also applied to Molech-worshipers who fall under Lev 20:2-5). This is DISTINCT from ordinary NEDER (H5088 voluntary vow of Lev 27:1-8). Jephthah vowed a נֶדֶר (Jdg 11:30 "wayyiddar Yiphtach NEDER laYHWH"), not a cherem. Therefore:
- Lev 27:1-8 (neder → always priest-redeemable via 'erek) applies to Jephthah's vow
- Lev 27:28-29 (cherem → non-redeemable, death-penalty) does NOT apply
- Jephthah had a Torah-sanctioned off-ramp (priestly valuation — 30 shekels for an adult female 20-60, or 10 shekels for a female 5-20) that he did not invoke

**Library:** `word-studies/H6187-erek.md`, `H2763-charam.md`, `H2764-cherem.md`.

## D. Vow Laws + Annulment — Numbers 30 (FULL CHAPTER)

> **Num 30:1** And Moses spake unto the heads of the tribes concerning the children of Israel, saying, This [is] the thing which the LORD hath commanded.
> **Num 30:2** If a man vow a vow unto the LORD, or swear an oath to bind his soul with a bond; he shall not break his word, he shall do according to all that proceedeth out of his mouth.
> **Num 30:3** If a woman also vow a vow unto the LORD, and bind [herself] by a bond, [being] in her father's house in her youth;
> **Num 30:4** And her father hear her vow, and her bond wherewith she hath bound her soul, and her father shall hold his peace at her: then all her vows shall stand, and every bond wherewith she hath bound her soul shall stand.
> **Num 30:5** But if her father disallow her in the day that he heareth; not any of her vows, or of her bonds wherewith she hath bound her soul, shall stand: and the LORD shall forgive her, because her father disallowed her.
> [vv. 6-15: husband's annulment rights; widows, divorcees]
> **Num 30:16** These [are] the statutes, which the LORD commanded Moses, between a man and his wife, between the father and his daughter, [being yet] in her youth in her father's house.

**Observation:** Num 30 governs A WOMAN'S OWN vow. Jephthah's case is different — HE made the vow, not his daughter. But Num 30:2 ("he shall do according to all that proceedeth out of his mouth") is the text Jephthah himself implicitly invokes at 11:35 with פָּצִיתִי פִי אֶל־יְהוָה ("I have opened my mouth").
