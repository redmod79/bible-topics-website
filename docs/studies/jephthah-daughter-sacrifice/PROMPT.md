# Bible Study: Did Jephthah Actually Sacrifice His Daughter, or Dedicate Her to Perpetual Virginity?

## Question

Did Jephthah actually sacrifice his daughter as a burnt offering (Judges 11:29-40), or did he dedicate her to perpetual virginity / lifelong temple service? What does the Hebrew text, the grammar of the vow, and the surrounding biblical theology indicate?

## Prior Research Summary

**v1 prior iteration** (`jephthah-daughter-sacrifice-v1`, 2026-04-16) concluded the weight of biblical evidence **modestly favors the perpetual-virginity / lifelong-dedication reading** over literal burnt-offering. v1's five main legs:

1. Narrator's lexical restraint at the climax (Jdg 11:39) — colorless `wayya`as lah et-nidro` avoids every available sacrifice verb (`zabach`, `shachat`, `saraph`, `alah` Hiphil, `he`evir baesh`) — contrast 2 Kgs 3:27, 16:3, 21:6, 23:10, Jer 19:5, Ezek 23:37-39, Gen 22:10.
2. `lo-yad`ah ish` (11:39) as WX-QATAL stative/durative — every other canonical occurrence describes *living* women's chastity (Gen 24:16; Num 31:17-18, 35; Jdg 21:11-12; 1 Kgs 1:4).
3. `bethulim` (H1331) mourning of *virginity-state*, not impending death — no OT parallel for anticipatory 2-month self-mourning (cf. Gen 50:10 Jacob 7d; Num 20:29 Aaron 30d).
4. `letannot` (H8567) Piel in Jdg 11:40 — dis legomenon; Jdg 5:11 control = "recount/celebrate" (`yetannu tzidqot YHWH`).
5. Torah's categorical prohibition + Jer 7:31 divine disavowal forecloses divinely-accepted sacrifice.

v1 conceded default syntax of Jdg 11:31 (`wehayah laYHWH weha`alitihu olah`) is conjunctive weqatal; `alah` Hiphil + `olah` is a technical cultic collocation (Gen 8:20; 22:13); Jephthah's `lo ukhal lashuv` (11:35) indicates he understood the vow as irrevocable.

**v1 gaps to close in v2:** (a) Hebrew-grammar argument lacked canonical grammar-reference entries on WX-QATAL disjunctive/circumstantial and weqatal-apodosis; (b) LXX cross-check on 11:31, 39, 40 not done systematically; (c) Targum Jonathan/Phinehas-redemption gloss not addressed; (d) `letannot` vs Jdg 5:11 LXX verb-choice not tested; (e) Jephthah Torah-literacy argument (Jdg 11:12-28 vs Num 20-21) not surfaced; (f) Heb 11:32-34 Greek aorist verbs not parsed and matched to specific Jephthah acts.

**External corpus signal** (from 00-references.md):
- **EGW silent** on the pericope — doctrine rests on Scripture alone (principle_scripture_authority).
- **Clarke Commentary** (28187, 28216, 28230, 28231, 28288): disjunctive-waw vow reading; moral-impossibility argument; Spirit-of-YHWH argument; **Targum Jonathan gloss** on 11:39 — "Jephthah did not consult Phinehas... who would have redeemed her with money" (literal-sacrifice view but Torah-violation framing).
- **Henry Commentary** (9409, 9411, 9412, 9414, 9976): surveys both; objects "no law or custom intimated celibacy as religion"; notes Num 30:5 father's annulment; connects 1 Sam 1:11 Hannah/Samuel as controlling parallel.
- **Edersheim BHOTV3 140.1, 141.1**: literal sacrifice; "praise" (not "lament") for `letannot`; rejects the "rough-times Torah-ignorant Jephthah" defense since Jdg 11:15-27 demonstrates Pentateuchal literacy.
- **Josephus Ant. 5.7.6, 5.7.10**: literal sacrifice; "neither conformable to the law nor acceptable to God"; paraphrases vow as "what living creature soever" (supports disjunctive-vow reading that vow anticipated any creature, not specifically a human).
- **SU silent**; **1 Meqabyan 4:30** — general Satanic condemnation of child sacrifice (minor lead, skip).
- **Sirach 18:22** — intertestamental vow-ethic pushed vow-payment absolutism (contextualizes Josephus' literal reading).
