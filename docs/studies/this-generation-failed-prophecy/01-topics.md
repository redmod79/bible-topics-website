# Topics Research

## Question
Jesus said "this generation shall not pass till all these things be fulfilled" (Matt 24:34, Mark 13:30, Luke 21:32) and "there be some standing here which shall not taste of death till they see the Son of man coming in his kingdom" (Matt 16:28, Mark 9:1, Luke 9:27). Some claim these are failed prophecies since Jesus did not return in the first century. Was this prophecy fulfilled according to His word?

## Topics

### PROPHECY
**Key sub-entries relevant to this study:**

**Sure fulfillment of prophecy:**
- EZK 12:22-25,28 — "The days are at hand, and the effect of every vision... the word that I shall speak shall come to pass; it shall be no more prolonged"
- HAB 2:3 — "the vision is yet for an appointed time... though it tarry, wait for it; because it will surely come"
- MAT 5:18 — "Till heaven and earth pass, one jot or one tittle shall in no wise pass from the law, till all be fulfilled"
- MAT 24:35 — "Heaven and earth shall pass away, but my words shall not pass away"
- ACT 13:27,29 — Jewish leaders fulfilled prophecy by condemning Christ

**Miscellaneous fulfilled prophecies** (extensive list including temple destruction, Jerusalem's fall, various historical events verified against prophetic word). The pattern: God's word does not fail; apparent "delays" have specific purposes.

### TRANSFIGURATION
- Of Moses: EXO 34:29-35
- Of Jesus: MAT 17:2-9
- Of Stephen: ACT 6:15

**Relevance:** The Transfiguration (MAT 17:1-9; MRK 9:2-8; LUK 9:28-36) immediately follows Jesus's "some standing here" statement in all three Synoptics. Peter explicitly links the Transfiguration to Jesus's "power and coming [parousia]" in 2 PET 1:16-18.

### KINGDOM OF HEAVEN
**Parables of the Kingdom:**
- Sower/wheat & tares: MAT 13:24-30,38-43; MRK 4:26-29
- Mustard seed: MAT 13:31,32; MRK 4:30,31; LUK 13:18,19
- Leaven: MAT 13:33; LUK 13:21
- Treasure/pearl: MAT 13:44,45
- Net: MAT 13:47-50
- King reckoning: MAT 18:23-35
- Ten virgins: MAT 25:1-13
- Talents: MAT 25:14-30

**Key statements:**
- "My kingdom is not of this world" — JHN 18:36
- Children of the kingdom — MAT 18:3; 19:14; MRK 10:14; LUK 18:16
- Keys of the kingdom — MAT 16:19
- Does not consist of meat and drink — ROM 14:17

**Relevance:** The "kingdom" language in Matt 16:28 ("see the Son of man coming in his kingdom"), Mark 9:1 ("kingdom of God come with power"), and Luke 9:27 ("see the kingdom of God") raises the question of whether the "kingdom" is a visible earthly return or a spiritual/heavenly reality that could be "seen" before the disciples' death.

### JERUSALEM (Destruction Section)
**Destruction foretold by Jesus:**
- MAT 23:37,38 — "Behold, your house is left unto you desolate"
- MAT 24:15 — Abomination of desolation standing in the holy place
- MRK 13:14-23 — Parallel account of the abomination
- LUK 13:35 — "your house is left unto you desolate"
- LUK 17:26-37 — Days of Noah/Lot parallel; judgment on "this generation"
- LUK 19:41-44 — Jesus weeps over Jerusalem; "enemies shall lay thee even with the ground... not leave in thee one stone upon another"
- LUK 21:20-24 — "When ye shall see Jerusalem compassed with armies... until the times of the Gentiles be fulfilled"

**OT Prophecies of Jerusalem's destruction:**
- DAN 9:24-27 — Seventy weeks; "the people of the prince that shall come shall destroy the city and the sanctuary"
- JER 7:32-34; 26:18; 32:29,31,32

**Relevance:** Jerusalem's destruction in AD 70 is widely recognized as partial fulfillment of the Olivet Discourse. The question is whether "all these things" (panta tauta) in Matt 24:34 includes only the events up through Jerusalem's fall or also the cosmic signs and parousia.

### JUDGMENT (THE GENERAL)
Key references establishing the final judgment as future:
- DAN 7:9,10 — Throne judgment scene (Ancient of Days)
- MAT 16:27 — "Son of man shall come in the glory of his Father... reward every man according to his works"
- MAT 25:1-46 — Parables of readiness; sheep and goats judgment
- MRK 13:32 — "of that day and that hour knoweth no man"
- 2PE 2:4,9; 3:7,10-12 — Judgment reserved for the future
- REV 1:7 — "he cometh with clouds; every eye shall see him"
- REV 20:11-15 — Great white throne judgment

**Relevance:** If the final judgment is still future, then the parousia cannot have occurred in AD 70, supporting the view that "these things" in Matt 24:34 does not include the Second Coming itself.

### VISION (Transfiguration Section)
- Peter, James, and John: Of the transfiguration of Jesus and the appearance of Moses and Elijah — MAT 17:1-9; LUK 9:28-36
- Of Daniel: Of the Ancient of Days — DAN 7:9-27

**Relevance:** Matt 17:9 calls the Transfiguration a "vision" (horama). This fits the pattern of prophetic vision as a preview of future reality, which may be how Peter understood the Transfiguration as evidence of Christ's parousia (2 Pet 1:16).

### JESUS, THE CHRIST — KINGDOM OF (Selected)
**Prophecies concerning universality of the kingdom:**
- DAN 7:13,14,18,22,27 — Son of man receives everlasting dominion
- MAT 16:28 listed among "unclassified prophecies concerning the kingdom"
- 2PE 1:11 — "everlasting kingdom of our Lord and Saviour Jesus Christ"

**Secular notions concerning the kingdom:**
- MAT 18:1-3; 20:20-23; JHN 6:15; ACT 1:6,7
- The disciples expected an earthly political kingdom; Jesus redirected this expectation
