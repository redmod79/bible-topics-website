## LEAD 1 — Bohr/Newton/Clarke/Froom: "Temple of God = Christian Church"

Verification route: (a) Pauline naos corpus pattern; (b) proximity of 2 Thess 51 AD to standing Herodian temple (literal counter-steelman); (c) Eph 2:19-22 + 1 Pet 2:4-5 building-metaphor chain cited by Bohr.

**Data:**
- Pauline naos pattern is 4-of-5 figurative: 1 Cor 3:16-17, 1 Cor 6:19, 2 Cor 6:16, Eph 2:21 all apply naos to the church or individual believers. 2 Thess 2:4 is the fifth Pauline token (disputed).
- Eph 2:19-22 — greek_parser.py output: "τὸν ναὸν τοῦ Θεοῦ" appears in 1 Cor 3:16-17; Eph 2:21 uses `εἰς ναὸν ἅγιον ἐν Κυρίῳ` — growing/organic building register with apostles/prophets as foundation, Christ as cornerstone (ἀκρογωνιαῖος).
- 1 Pet 2:4-5 — "lively stones, spiritual house" (`οἶκος πνευματικός`) — parallel but uses οἶκος not ναός.
- 2 Thess dating: ~51 AD (Paul's second missionary journey). Herodian temple stood until AD 70. Caligula statue-crisis was AD 40.
- Matt 24:15 Olivet citation (by Jesus, ~30 AD) presupposes a future abomination in the holy place — the first Christian audience read 2 Thess 2:4 through Olivet.

**Biblical weight of the claim:**
- PRO figurative: Pauline author-internal pattern strongly confirmed; Eph 2:21 + 1 Pet 2:5 parallel stream; Heb 3:6 "whose house are we"; Acts 7:47-50 "God dwelleth not in houses made with hands."
- PRO literal: the 51-AD living-temple; the Caligula precedent; Matt 24:15 pendant; Rev 11:1-2 measured naos.
- Status: both readings have biblical warrant; no single datum forces the choice. Analysis agent must weigh author-consistency-principle against historical-proximity.

## LEAD 2 — Bohr's "Two-phase papal rule with deadly-wound interval"

Claim: The man of sin rules for 1260 years, receives a deadly wound (Rev 13:3), interval, heals, returns to rule again.

Verification route: Does 2 Thess 2 itself support two-phase reading, or is this imported from Rev 13:3?

**Data:**
- 2 Thess 2:6-8 gives a four-stage timeline: (1) mystery-already-works; (2) restrainer-removed; (3) revealed; (4) destroyed by parousia.
- No textual datum in 2 Thess 2 inserts a "wound interval" between stages 3 and 4.
- The "wound" concept is uniquely Rev 13:3 (`μίαν ἐκ τῶν κεφαλῶν αὐτοῦ ὡς ἐσφαγμένην εἰς θάνατον, καὶ ἡ πληγὴ τοῦ θανάτου αὐτοῦ ἐθεραπεύθη`).

**Biblical weight:**
- 2 Thess 2 alone supports a single-arc progression (already-now → revealed → destroyed). Two-phase reading is a Rev-13 overlay, not a 2 Thess 2 text-derivation.
- This does NOT make the two-phase reading wrong — Rev 13:3 is canonical — but it DOES mean the two-phase claim is NOT derivable from 2 Thess 2's own text. It is a Revelation-derived hermeneutical extension.

## LEAD 3 — Matthew Henry: Dan 11:36-37 → 1 Tim 4:1-3 → papal celibacy / Mahuzzim saints

Claim: "Nor shall he regard the desire of women" (Dan 11:37) = celibacy; "god of forces" (Mahuzzim, Dan 11:38) = saint-veneration; 1 Tim 4:3 "forbidding to marry" = the same apostasy the Antichrist embodies.

Verification route: (a) Hebrew of Dan 11:37 `chemdat nashim`; (b) Hebrew of Dan 11:38 `ma'uzzim`; (c) Greek of 1 Tim 4:3 `κωλυόντων γαμεῖν` vs. 2 Thess 2:3 ἀποστασία.

**Data:**
- **Hebrew `chemdat nashim`** (Dan 11:37) — literally "desire of women." Semantic range: (i) natural affection for women / marriage bond; (ii) one specific desired woman (a lover/queen); (iii) by interpretive extension, abandoning normal sexual life. The Hebrew does NOT lexically entail celibacy-system; that reading is an interpretive possibility among several.
- **Hebrew `ma'uzzim`** (Dan 11:38) — plural of ma'ōz "fortress, stronghold." Primary sense is military (god-of-fortresses). The saint/patron-veneration reading is by interpretive extension; the Hebrew does not itself denote saints.
- **1 Tim 4:3 `κωλυόντων γαμεῖν καὶ ἀπέχεσθαι βρωμάτων`** — forbidding to marry and commanding abstinence from foods. Explicit "forbidding marriage" register.
- The Dan-11 `chemdat nashim` → 1 Tim 4:3 `κωλύοντες γαμεῖν` lexical bridge requires interpretive assumptions (Dan 11's phrase → broader celibacy meaning; 1 Tim 4's phrase applied to the Dan-11 figure). Neither bridge is linguistically necessary; both are interpretively available.

**Biblical weight:**
- Dan 11:37-38 is semantically compatible with the celibacy/saint-veneration reading; it does NOT demand it.
- 1 Tim 4:1-3 explicitly describes a latter-times apostasy with forbid-marriage/abstain-meat marks, providing a content-specification that CAN be applied to the Dan-11 figure.
- The *fusion* of the two passages into a single identification rests on (a) reading 2 Thess 2:3 as the 1 Tim 4:1 apostasia; (b) reading Dan 11:36-37 as the 2 Thess 2:4 figure; (c) importing 1 Tim 4:3's content-marks into Dan 11's description. All three steps are biblically plausible; none is biblically forced.

## LEAD 4 — Guinness's "commanded sin" argument

Claim: anomia in 2 Thess 2 has institutional / commanded-sin register, not mere personal wickedness. This is what makes the man of sin identifiably the papal system rather than any generic wicked person.

Verification route: Does G458 anomia lexically/semantically connote organized/institutional opposition to law?

**Data:**
- G458 anomia Louw-Nida: 88.18 "Act Lawlessly."
- Range across NT: (a) concrete deeds ("iniquities" plural; Rom 4:7, Heb 8:12, Titus 2:14, Heb 10:17); (b) character/moral condition (Matt 23:28; 2 Cor 6:14 antithesis; Heb 1:9); (c) lawlessness personified in agents (Matt 7:23, 13:41; Rom 6:19); (d) eschatological intensification (Matt 24:12; 2 Thess 2:3, 7).
- Dan 7:25 (Aramaic) — "think to change times and laws" (`y-hashnāyāh zimnīn v-dāt`). The little horn's programmatic law-opposition is institutional, not individual. This OT template supports a reading of anomia as organized/systemic.
- 1 Jn 3:4 — `ἡ ἁμαρτία ἐστὶν ἡ ἀνομία` — equates sin and lawlessness; suggests they are not rigidly distinguished as individual-vs-institutional.

**Biblical weight:**
- Guinness's reading is *compatible* with NT anomia range. The Dan 7:25 institutional-law-opposition template strongly supports it.
- But the NT does not *enforce* an institutional-only reading — anomia ranges across individual/moral/eschatological scales. The Guinness argument is an available exegetical move, not a forced one.

## LEAD 5 — Newton/Clarke: "Pope in general, not any one pope" — succession vs. individual

Claim: Greek ὁ ἄνθρωπος τῆς ἀνομίας + definite article + singular does not force a single historical person; it allows a representative-of-class reading (pope-as-type, succession-as-one).

Verification route: (a) Greek anthrōpos + article + genitive construction grammatical range; (b) Dan 7:17 "four kings" = "four kingdoms" LXX precedent; (c) NT parallels where singular anthrōpos names a corporate or typological figure.

**Data:**
- G444 anthrōpos — 557 NT tokens; base meaning "human being" generically.
- With attributive genitive ("man of X"): denotes (i) individual characterized; (ii) class/type; or (iii) corporate/representative figure (per grammar-ref `$GRAMMAR_REF/greek/anthropos-individual-vs-type.md`).
- Examples of corporate/typological anthrōpos in Paul:
  - Rom 5:12 "by one man (δι' ἑνὸς ἀνθρώπου) sin entered" — individual who is a corporate head.
  - 1 Cor 15:21 "by man came death, by man came resurrection" — two-Adam typology.
  - 1 Cor 15:45, 47 "first man Adam … last Adam / second man" — individual-as-type.
  - Rom 6:6 "old man / new man" — corporate aspectual identity.
- Dan 7:17 LXX — `τὰ τέσσαρα θηρία … τέσσαρες βασιλεῖς` ("four kings"). v.23 translates this as "four kingdoms" (`τέσσαρα βασιλεῖα`). **The text itself says "four kings" = "four kingdoms."** This is the single explicit biblical precedent for king-singular-equals-kingdom-collective.
- Dan 2 — "thou art the head of gold" (Nebuchadnezzar personally) followed by "after thee shall rise another kingdom" (collective kingdom). Same pattern: a personal king standing for a kingdom-collective.

**Biblical weight:**
- The grammar alone (ὁ ἄνθρωπος + singular) does NOT force individual reading; anthrōpos-as-corporate-representative is a biblical pattern.
- Daniel itself (7:17 → 7:23) establishes the precedent.
- The Newton/Clarke succession reading is thus grammatically and biblically available; it is NOT foreclosed by anthrōpos singular.
- Equally, the grammar does not force the succession reading — it allows both.

## LEAD 7 — LXX Dan 7:8 verbatim at Rev 13:5 — extend to 2 Thess 2:4 vs. Dan 11:36 / Isa 14 / Ezek 28

Claim: The verbatim LXX-tracer showing Rev 13:5 = Dan 7:8 LXX (`στόμα λαλοῦν μεγάλα`) should be run on 2 Thess 2:4.

Verification:
- **LXX Theod. Dan 11:36** — `ὑψωθήσεται καὶ μεγαλυνθήσεται ἐπὶ πάντα θεόν` ("he shall be exalted and magnified above every god").
- **2 Thess 2:4** — `ὑπεραιρόμενος ἐπὶ πάντα λεγόμενον Θεὸν ἢ σέβασμα` ("exalting himself above every so-called god or object of worship").
- Shared Greek: `ἐπὶ πάντα θεόν` (verbatim preposition + accusative + noun). Paul adds `λεγόμενον` ("so-called") as qualifier and `ἢ σέβασμα` as expansion.
- Also: the LXX-Theod pair `ὑψωθήσεται καὶ μεγαλυνθήσεται` (fut. pass./mid.) is semantically matched by Paul's middle participle `ὑπεραιρόμενος` (middle-reflexive).
- **LXX Isa 14:13-14** — `ἀναβήσομαι εἰς τὸν οὐρανόν … καθιῶ ἐν ὄρει ὑψηλῷ … ὅμοιος ἔσομαι τῷ ὑψίστῳ` ("I will ascend into heaven … I will sit upon a lofty mountain … I will be like the Most High"). The `καθιῶ` (sit-motif) + divine-comparison register is conceptually shared with 2 Thess 2:4 `εἰς τὸν ναὸν τοῦ Θεοῦ καθίσαι` + `ὅτι ἔστιν Θεός`.
- **LXX Ezek 28:2** — `θεὸς ἐγώ εἰμι, κατοικίαν θεοῦ κατῴκηκα ἐν καρδίᾳ θαλάσσης` ("I am God, I inhabit a dwelling of God in the heart of the seas"). The `θεὸς ἐγώ εἰμι` claim is conceptually the direct antecedent to 2 Thess 2:4 `ὅτι ἔστιν Θεός`.

**Verbal verdict:**
- 2 Thess 2:4 `ἐπὶ πάντα … θεόν` — verbatim LXX-Theod Dan 11:36.
- 2 Thess 2:4 `καθίσαι` in divine seat — conceptually (not verbatim) Isa 14:13 `καθιῶ` and Ezek 28:2 `κατοικίαν θεοῦ κατῴκηκα`.
- 2 Thess 2:4 `ὅτι ἔστιν Θεός` — conceptual (not verbatim) Ezek 28:2 `θεὸς ἐγώ εἰμι`.

**Weight:**
Dan 11:36 is the primary verbatim OT template for 2 Thess 2:4; Isa 14 and Ezek 28 are thematic templates without verbatim overlap. This confirms Paul's direct dependence on the Dan-11 self-exalting-king portrait.

## OBSERVATIONS ON THE EIGHT-MARKER TABLE

(1) Historicist view FITS all 8 markers without explicit FAIL but requires the figurative-naos reading.
(2) Preterist has explicit FAIL at marker 7 (destruction by parousia) and STRAIN at markers 1 and 8. Neither Nero nor Jewish high-priesthood nor Antiochus was destroyed by "the brightness of the Lord's coming."
(3) Dispensational Futurist FITS most markers but STRAINs markers 5 (Holy-Spirit-as-restrainer requires rapture-removal doctrine not in the text) and 6 (single-future-figure vs. "already working").
(4) Idealist FITS 1 and 3 and 6 but STRAINs on singularity markers (ἀποκαλυφθῆ singular subject; κριθῶσιν plural; single destruction).
(5) Roman-emperor view has explicit FAIL at markers 1, 7, and 8. A pagan emperor cannot be the ho anthrōpos who FOLLOWS an ἀποστασία, cannot be destroyed by the brightness of the parousia, and cannot parallel Rev 17's multi-century MYSTERY-BABYLON.

**The preterist and Roman-emperor views have explicit textual FAILs. The other three views have only STRAIN-type tension, each resolvable by additional exegetical moves.**
