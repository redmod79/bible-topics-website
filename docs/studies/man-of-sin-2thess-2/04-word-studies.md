## A. The Five Titles of the Figure

A single referent bears five distinct designations across vv.3, 4, 8, 9. Cross-linked by anaphoric articles and a relative pronoun. See passage analysis at `$GRAMMAR_REF/passages/2thess-2-1-12.md`.

| # | Greek | Verse | Parsing | Lexemes | Semantic register | Candidate referent direction |
|---|-------|-------|---------|---------|-------------------|---------|
| 1 | **ὁ ἄνθρωπος τῆς ἀνομίας** | 2:3 | T-NSM + N-NSM + T-GSF + N-GSF | G444 anthrōpos + G458 anomia (N1904) or G266 hamartia (TR/KJV) | "the human characterized by lawlessness (or sin)" — attributive genitive of quality | Human-category figure defined by anomia; ambiguous individual/class/succession per anthrōpos-individual-vs-type grammar |
| 2 | **ὁ υἱὸς τῆς ἀπωλείας** | 2:3 | T-NSM + N-NSM + T-GSF + N-GSF | G5207 huios + G684 apōleia | "the son of perdition/destruction" — Semitic destiny-genitive | Paralleled only at Jn 17:12 (Judas) and chains to Rev 17:8, 11 (beast) via ἀπώλεια |
| 3 | **ὁ ἀντικείμενος καὶ ὑπεραιρόμενος** | 2:4 | T-NSM + V-PNP-NSM + Conj + V-PPP-NSM | G480 antikeimai (mid/pass dep) + G5229 hyperairō (middle) | "the one opposing and exalting himself" — paired middle participles | Active self-positioning against God/sebasma; reflexive-self register |

## B. Lawlessness Vocabulary Cluster (G458 / G459 / G266)

### G458 ἀνομία (anomia) — 15 tokens / 13 verses; 7 books
**Source:** `$WORD_STUDIES/G458-anomia.md`

- Etymology: α- (privative) + νόμος (law) — literally "not-law."
- Louw-Nida 88.18 "Act Lawlessly."
- KJV distribution: "iniquity" 7x (53.8%); "iniquities" 3x (23.1%); "transgression of the law" 1x (7.7%).
- Distribution by book: Matthew (4), Hebrews (3), Romans (2), 2 Thess (1, v.7 + variant v.3), Titus (1), 2 Cor (1), 1 Jn (1).
- Pauline/eschatological uses: Titus 2:14 "redeem us from all iniquity"; 2 Thess 2:3 (variant), 2:7 `τὸ μυστήριον τῆς ἀνομίας`.
- **1 Jn 3:4** — the definitional equation: "sin is the transgression of the law" (`ἡ ἁμαρτία ἐστὶν ἡ ἀνομία`). Only NT text explicitly equating G266 and G458.
- Matt 24:12 — "iniquity shall abound" (πληθυνθῆναι τὴν ἀνομίαν) — eschatological intensification matching 2 Thess 2:7.
- Hebrew source (LXX): primarily עָוֺן H5771 (70 co-occurrences, PMI 22.69) and פֶּשַׁע H6588 (29, PMI 17.93).

### G459 ἄνομος (anomos) — 10 tokens / 7 verses; 7 books
**Source:** `$WORD_STUDIES/G459-anomos.md`

- Adjective; compound α- + νόμος.
- Louw-Nida 88.18 (also 11.002 for Gentile sense).
- Semantic range: (a) Gentile/outside-Torah (neutral — 1 Cor 9:21); (b) morally lawless/wicked (1 Tim 1:9); (c) substantival "the lawless one" (2 Thess 2:8 — THE only articular-singular substantival use in NT).
- Morphological distribution: 6 plural "transgressors / lawless men"; 2 Cor-one-4× "without law"; 2 Pet 2:8 attribute "unlawful deeds"; 2 Thess 2:8 sole articular-singular-substantival.
- Hebrew source (LXX): רָשָׁע rasha H7563 (28 co-occurrences, PMI 4.77), פָּשַׁע pasha H6586 (Isa 53:12 citation at Mark 15:28 / Lk 22:37).
- KJV 2 Thess 2:8 rendering: "that Wicked" (capitalized) — reflects articular-titular force.

### G266 ἁμαρτία (hamartia) — ~173 tokens
**Source:** `$WORD_STUDIES/G266-hamartia.md`

- Feminine noun from ἁμαρτάνω G264 ("to miss the mark").
- Louw-Nida 88.289 — Sin, Wrongdoing, Guilt.
- KJV: "sin/sins/of sin" — 100% cluster.
- Densest books: 1 John (17), Romans (48), Hebrews (24).
- The Byzantine/TR reading at 2 Thess 2:3 (τῆς ἁμαρτίας) vs. the Alexandrian (τῆς ἀνομίας) — see the textual-variant grammar entry.

### G460 ἀνόμως (anomōs) — adverbial, Rom 2:12 only
- Covered implicitly under G459.

### Summary — The anomia/anomos/hamartia cluster in 2 Thess 2

| Verse | Form | Gloss | Referent |
|-------|------|-------|----------|
| 2:3 (N1904) | τῆς ἀνομίας G458 | "of lawlessness" | descriptive genitive of the anthrōpos |
| 2:3 (TR/KJV) | τῆς ἁμαρτίας G266 | "of sin" | descriptive genitive of the anthrōpos |
| 2:7 | τῆς ἀνομίας G458 | "of the lawlessness" | descriptive genitive of τὸ μυστήριον |
| 2:8 | ὁ ἄνομος G459 | "the lawless one" | substantival adjective — the same figure |

**Observation:** Whether one reads the variant at v.3 as `ἀνομίας` or `ἁμαρτίας`, the final title at v.8 (`ὁ ἄνομος` G459) is UN-varying. The anomia-word-family dominates the pericope whether or not the v.3 variant is adopted.

## C. Restrainer Vocabulary (G2722 katechō) — CRITICAL

**Source:** `$WORD_STUDIES/G2722-katecho.md`

- 18 NT tokens / 18 verses / 10 books; heavily Pauline (11 of 18).
- Compound κατά + ἔχω.
- Louw-Nida 037.001 (restrain), 057 (possess), 031.006 (hold a view), 085 (occupy a place), 054 (maritime).
- Lexical versatility: "keep / hold fast / possess / hold back / detain / occupy / head toward."
- 2 Thess 2 occurrences BOTH ONE-OFF and are the ONLY articular-substantival-participle uses in the NT.

### The 2 Thess 2:6-7 Gender Shift

| Verse | Greek | Article | Parsing | KJV |
|-------|-------|---------|---------|-----|
| 2:6 | τὸ κατέχον | τό (NEUTER) | V-PAP-ASN | "what withholdeth" |
| 2:7 | ὁ κατέχων | ὁ (MASCULINE) | V-PAP-NSM | "he who now letteth" |

- Same verb, same tense, same voice, same mood — only gender + case change.
- Lexical force in both: *detainment / hindrance* — the restrainer holds back the *revelation* of the lawless one, not his *truth*.

### Restrainer Candidate Catalogue

From grammar-reference `$GRAMMAR_REF/greek/concord-shift-neuter-to-masculine.md` (context-neutral):

| # | Proposed Referent | Neuter v.6 encodes | Masculine v.7 encodes |
|---|-------------------|-------------------|----------------------|
| 1 | Holy Spirit | πνεῦμα (neuter) | Spirit as Person (cf. John 14:26; 16:13 where neuter τὸ πνεῦμα governs masculine ἐκεῖνος) |
| 2 | Roman Empire / Roman Emperor | imperium / the state (neuter) | The Emperor (masculine) |
| 3 | Angelic restrainer (Michael) | angelic restraining office (neuter) | Michael as personal prince (Dan 10:13, 21; 12:1) |
| 4 | Gospel preaching / the preacher | τὸ εὐαγγέλιον / τὸ κήρυγμα (neuter) | the preacher (Paul, Christ, or herald) |
| 5 | God's sovereign restraint / God as Person | divine restraining purpose (neuter) | God as personal Restrainer (masculine) |
| 6 | Self-restraint of the man of lawlessness | stative condition of being held back (neuter) | the man himself, held back until his ἀποκάλυψις (masculine) |

**What the grammar alone does NOT decide:** which entity is the restrainer; whether two referents or one referent in two aspects. Grammar alone is underdetermined — context must decide.

## D. Temporal-Modal Chain

| Stage | Greek | Verse | Parsing | Temporal marker | Event |
|-------|-------|-------|---------|-----------------|-------|
| 1. Already-in-operation | τὸ γὰρ μυστήριον **ἤδη ἐνεργεῖται** τῆς ἀνομίας | 2:7 | V-PMI-3S (G1754) | **ἤδη** "already" | Lawlessness-mystery presently at work |
| 2. Removal of restrainer | **ἕως ἐκ μέσου γένηται** | 2:7 | V-2ADS-3S (G1096) | **ἕως** "until" | ὁ κατέχων removed from midst |
| 3. Revelation of lawless one | καὶ τότε **ἀποκαλυφθήσεται** ὁ ἄνομος | 2:8 | V-FPI-3S (G601) | **καὶ τότε** "and then" | Fut. pass. — lawless one unveiled |
| 4a. Destruction (consume) | ὃν ὁ Κύριος **ἀνελεῖ** τῷ πνεύματι τοῦ στόματος αὐτοῦ | 2:8 | V-2FAI-3S (G337) | simultaneous/culminating | By the breath of his mouth (Isa 11:4) |
| 4b. Destruction (render inoperative) | καὶ **καταργήσει** τῇ ἐπιφανείᾳ τῆς παρουσίας αὐτοῦ | 2:8 | V-FAI-3S (G2673) | simultaneous | By the brightness of his coming |

**Inclusio of ἀποκαλύπτω G601** across three grammatical stances:
- v.3 — Aor. Pass. Subj. (ἀποκαλυφθῇ) — irrealis, within ἐὰν-μή conditional.
- v.6 — Aor. Pass. Inf. (ἀποκαλυφθῆναι) — purpose/result.
- v.8 — Fut. Pass. Ind. (ἀποκαλυφθήσεται) — realis prediction.
All three have the lawless one as subject. Progression: contingent → purposed → predicted. See `$WORD_STUDIES/G601-apokalypto.md`.

## E. Divine Passive Chain

All three ἀποκαλύπτω forms above are **passive** without expressed agent — candidate divine passives. See `$GRAMMAR_REF/greek/divine-passive.md`. God is the implicit unveiler, paralleling Christ's own ἀποκάλυψις in 2 Thess 1:7 (`ἐν τῇ ἀποκαλύψει τοῦ Κυρίου Ἰησοῦ`).

The `ἐδόθη` (it-was-given) chain in Rev 13:5, 7 operates on the same principle — Rev 13 gives the beast authority by passive, God being the implicit permitter. Daniel's `y-hiv` (Aramaic passive "was given"; Dan 7:25 `y-hiv-vūn` "they shall be given into his hand") continues the same divine-permission register. The system across these three passages consistently marks God as the sovereign allower of temporary evil-power.

## F. Apostasia + Article + Precondition (G646)

**Source:** `$WORD_STUDIES/G646-apostasia.md`

- 2 NT tokens: Acts 21:21 (anarthrous accusative — "apostasy from Moses") and 2 Thess 2:3 (articular nominative — `ἡ ἀποστασία`).
- Louw-Nida 39.34 — Rebellion (NOT "departure" in the mild sense; lexicographically in the insurrection cluster).
- LXX: used for Hebrew מַעַל ma'al (covenantal treachery; primary), מְשׁוּבָה (backsliding), מֶרֶד (rebellion).
- **1 Macc 2:15** — `τὴν ἀποστασίαν` of the Antiochene persecution: specific, named, historically-climactic covenant-breaking — the ONE Second Temple parallel to articular ἡ ἀποστασία.
- Verbal cognate G868 ἀφίστημι at **1 Tim 4:1** `ἀποστήσονταί τινες τῆς πίστεως` — defines the apostasia's content as departure from faith (seducing spirits, doctrines of devils, forbidding marriage, abstaining from meats).

**The article ἡ in 2 Thess 2:3:**
The article is grammatically significant but **does not itself** decide whether the referent is (a) a specific future climactic apostasy; (b) a specific apostasy Paul's readers knew from prior teaching; or (c) the generic/abstract category. Anaphoric possibility is specifically enabled by 2 Thess 2:5 — "Remember ye not, that, when I was yet with you, I told you these things?" — indicating prior oral teaching now not recoverable.

**Precondition logic:** `ἐὰν μή + aor.subj. … πρῶτον` — "unless come first." Temporal ordering: apostasia PRECEDES the revelation of the man of sin. A still-future apostasia reading; a present-operative apostasia reading; and a past-historical apostasia reading all cohere with the grammar.

## G. Pauline naos Usage Table — Decides Literal vs. Figurative

**Source:** `$WORD_STUDIES/G3485-naos.md`

| Reference | Phrase | Referent | Explicit indwelling |
|-----------|--------|---------|-------------------|
| 1 Cor 3:16 | `ναὸς Θεοῦ ἐστε` | believers collectively | Spirit of God dwelleth in you |
| 1 Cor 3:17 | `τὸν ναὸν τοῦ Θεοῦ ... ὁ ναὸς τοῦ Θεοῦ ἅγιός ἐστιν` | believers collectively | "which [temple] ye are" |
| 1 Cor 6:19 | `ναὸς τοῦ ἐν ὑμῖν Ἁγίου Πνεύματος` | individual body | Holy Spirit in you |
| 2 Cor 6:16 | `ἡμεῖς ... ναὸς Θεοῦ ἐσμεν ζῶντος` | believers collectively | "I will dwell in them" |
| Eph 2:21 | `εἰς ναὸν ἅγιον ἐν Κυρίῳ` | growing apostolic-prophetic-founded community | "habitation of God through the Spirit" (2:22) |
| 2 Thess 2:4 | `εἰς τὸν ναὸν τοῦ Θεοῦ` | **DISPUTED** | — (no explicit indwelling specified) |

**Paul's own pattern (4 of 5 tokens):** The `ναός` / `οἰκ-` / dwelling-metaphor cluster consistently applies to the believing community. The ONLY Pauline use whose referent is debated is 2 Thess 2:4.

**Principle of Pauline consistency** (Scripture-interprets-Scripture within author): author's own pattern is strongest intra-corpus evidence. FOUR data points vs. ONE disputed.

**Steelman for literal reading** (contextually available):
- 2 Thess 51 AD — Herodian temple standing.
- Caligula AD 40 statue-crisis in living memory of Paul's readers.
- Matt 24:15 future-looking abomination prediction still lived within prophetic horizon.
- Rev 11:1-2 naos is measured apocalyptically (splits inner naos from outer court at 42 months).

**Related non-Pauline naos data:**
- Jn 2:19-21 — Christ's body IS naos (figurative use is christologically grounded).
- 1 Pet 2:4-5 — "spiritual house" (οἶκος πνευματικός) living-stones — parallel building-metaphor though not naos.
- Acts 7:47-50 — "God dwelleth not in houses made with hands" (Stephen, directly bearing on a literal-location reading).
- Rev 21:22 — no naos in the eschatological city; God and Lamb ARE the naos.

## H. Son-of-X Semitic Idiom (G5207 huios)

**Source:** `$WORD_STUDIES/G5207-huios.md` + `$WORD_STUDIES/TR-ben-huios.md` + `$GRAMMAR_REF/greek/son-of-X-semitic-idiom.md`

- `ὁ υἱὸς τῆς ἀπωλείας` in NT occurs ONLY at Jn 17:12 (Judas) and 2 Thess 2:3.
- Semitic "son of X" — Hebrew *ben-X* construct — denotes characterization and/or destiny (not biological descent).
- Parallels: `υἱοὶ τῆς ἀπειθείας` "sons of disobedience" (Eph 2:2, 5:6); `υἱοὶ φωτός` "sons of light" (1 Thess 5:5); `τέκνα ὀργῆς` "children of wrath" (Eph 2:3).
- With definite article + genitive = destiny/quasi-title construction.
- Routes into the Rev 17:8, 11 `ὑπάγει εἰς ἀπώλειαν` chain via the shared lexeme G684 (`ἀπώλεια`).

**Judas parallel register (Jn 17:12):**
- Insider who fell away; the only member of the twelve who was "lost."
- Destruction pre-determined in scripture (Ps 41:9 echo).
- 2 Thess 2:3 man of sin therefore inherits a betrayer-from-within paradigm.

## I. ἐνέργεια Subjective Genitive (G1753 / G1754)

**Source:** `$WORD_STUDIES/G1753-energeia.md`, G1754-energeo.md; `$GRAMMAR_REF/greek/energeia-subjective-genitive.md`.

- G1753 ἐνέργεια — 8 NT tokens, all Pauline.
- G1754 ἐνεργέω — 22 tokens, 12 books; voice theologically significant (active for personal agents; middle for impersonal forces).
- In every case the genitive identifies the SOURCE/AGENT of working, not the object.

**Three ἐνέργεια subjects in 2 Thess 2:**
- v.7 `τὸ μυστήριον ἤδη ἐνεργεῖται τῆς ἀνομίας` — middle voice; the mystery is self-energizing.
- v.9 `κατ' ἐνέργειαν τοῦ Σατανᾶ` — subjective genitive: Satan is agent.
- v.11 `πέμπει αὐτοῖς ὁ Θεὸς ἐνέργειαν πλάνης` — God the subject who sends; πλάνης is attributive (kind of working).

The ἐνεργ- root thus moves across THREE agents (mystery, Satan, God) — the judicial-passive register God exhibits when releasing delusion upon those who rejected the love of truth.

## J. Paired Middle Participles (antikeimai + hyperairō)

**Source:** `$GRAMMAR_REF/greek/paired-middle-participles.md` + G480-antikeimai.md + G5229-hyperairō.md

- `ὁ ἀντικείμενος καὶ ὑπεραιρόμενος` in 2 Thess 2:4 — TWO present middle/passive participles coordinated by καί under one article (ὁ).
- **G480 antikeimai:** 8 NT tokens; deponent middle/passive; 6 of 8 forms are participles used substantivally; 2 Thess 2:4 is the ONLY singular-masculine articular-substantival form in the NT.
- **G5229 hyperairō:** 3 NT tokens, 2 verses — 2 Cor 12:7 (×2, of Paul himself — pride danger) and 2 Thess 2:4.
- Paired middle register intensifies the self-focused character: "setting-oneself-in-opposition + raising-oneself-above."
- Preposition `ἐπί + acc.` marks WHAT the figure raises himself over: `πάντα λεγόμενον Θεὸν ἢ σέβασμα` ("every so-called god or object-of-worship").

## K. Reflexive + ὅτι Display (G584 apodeiknymi)

**Source:** `$WORD_STUDIES/G584-apodeiknymi.md` + `$GRAMMAR_REF/greek/reflexive-hoti-display.md`

- G584 apodeiknymi — only 4 NT tokens, all different tense/voice/mood.
- Acts 2:22 — perfect passive participle "having been demonstrated / accredited" (of Christ, by God's miracles).
- 2 Thess 2:4 — present active participle "displaying" (of the lawless one, by his own self-display).
- **Direct counter-mirror:** Acts 2:22 — God publicly accredits Jesus as Lord/Christ by signs; 2 Thess 2:4 — the man of sin displays himself as God by his own claim. Same verb, opposite direction of agency.
- `ἀποδεικνύντα ἑαυτὸν ὅτι ἔστιν Θεός` — the ὅτι-clause is the content of the display speech-act: the figure "shows himself [to be such] that he is God."
- Textual variant: TR adds `ὡς Θεόν` ("as God") before `καθίσαι`, making the claim more explicit. KJV preserves.

## L. Deception Cluster (G4106 planē, G4105 planaō, G539 apatē)

**Source:** `$WORD_STUDIES/G4106-plane.md` + G4105-planao.md

- Direct antonym with ἀλήθεια (truth) — cf. 1 Jn 4:6 `πνεῦμα τῆς ἀληθείας / πνεῦμα τῆς πλάνης`.

**G4105 πλανάω (verb) in Matt 24 parallel:** Matt 24:4-5, 11, 24 `μή τις ὑμᾶς πλανήσῃ`; "false prophets shall rise, and shall deceive many"; "shall shew great signs and wonders; insomuch that, if it were possible, they shall deceive the very elect." Lexical bridge between Olivet-deception and 2 Thess 2:9-11.

## M. Signs + Wonders (G4592 sēmeion + G5059 teras)

**Source:** G4592-semeion.md + G5059-teras.md

## N. Destruction Verbs (G337 anaireō + G2673 katargeō)

**Source:** G337-anaireo.md + `$WORD_STUDIES/G2673-katargeo.md`

### G337 ἀναιρέω
- 24 tokens / 23 verses / 5 books. 79% in Acts (Lukan narrative verb for killing). 83% KJV "kill/slay/put to death."
- Acts: Herod slaying Bethlehem children; plots to kill Christ; Paul persecution-era killings; executions.
- Heb 10:9 — rare "abolish/take away" sense.
- Acts 7:21 — rare "take up/adopt" sense (Pharaoh's daughter taking up Moses).
- 2 Thess 2:8 — the lone Pauline use; future active indicative; object = ὁ ἄνομος.

### G2673 καταργέω
- Pauline eschatological abolition verb; "render inoperative / nullify / destroy."
- Used of death being abolished (1 Cor 15:26), the law's condemning function abolished (Rom 7:2), every rule and authority abolished at the end (1 Cor 15:24).
- 2 Thess 2:8 — future active; complement to ἀνελεῖ.

**Doublet 2 Thess 2:8 destruction pattern:**
- ἀνελεῖ (remove-by-killing) + καταργήσει (render-inoperative): semantic redundancy emphasizes total elimination.
- Two dative-of-means phrases: `τῷ πνεύματι τοῦ στόματος αὐτοῦ` (Isa 11:4 echo) + `τῇ ἐπιφανείᾳ τῆς παρουσίας αὐτοῦ` (Christological climax).
- The TR variant `ἀναλώσει` (G355 analiskō "consume, use up") is a lexical synonym; no voice/tense/mood change.

## O. Parousia + Epiphaneia (G3952 + G2015)

**Source:** G3952-parousia.md + G2015-epiphaneia.md

- G3952 παρουσία — "presence, arrival, coming." Used OF CHRIST in 2 Thess 2:1, 8; 1 Cor 15:23; Matt 24:3; etc. Used OF THE LAWLESS ONE in 2 Thess 2:9. This **lexical collision is deliberate** — the figure's coming is the counter-mirror of Christ's.
- G2015 ἐπιφάνεια — "visible manifestation / appearing." Six NT tokens, all Pauline Pastorals + 2 Thess. Refers to Christ's appearings (1 Tim 6:14; 2 Tim 1:10; 4:1, 8; Tit 2:13; 2 Thess 2:8).
- **Doublet `τῇ ἐπιφανείᾳ τῆς παρουσίας`** — uniquely here. Compounds the visible-manifestation noun with the advent-noun. It is the radiance-of-the-coming that destroys. Genitive of source (ἐπιφάνεια belongs to / emerges from / is-of the παρουσία).

## P. mysterion (G3466) — mystery of iniquity vs. mystery of godliness

**Source:** `$WORD_STUDIES/G3466-mysterion.md`

- 27 NT verses; heavily Pauline prison epistles (Col 4, Eph 6); 2 Thess 1; 1 Tim 2; Rev 4.
- Louw-Nida 28.005 — Not Able To Be Known, Secret.
- **Antithesis: 1 Tim 3:16 `τὸ τῆς εὐσεβείας μυστήριον` vs. 2 Thess 2:7 `τὸ μυστήριον τῆς ἀνομίας`.** Paul's "mystery" register has two opposite poles: the mystery of godliness (Christ in the flesh) vs. the mystery of iniquity. Both are μυστήριον + attributive genitive.
- Rev 17:5 `ΜΥΣΤΗΡΙΟΝ, ΒΑΒΥΛΩΝ Η ΜΕΓΑΛΗ` — same word, title-inscription of the harlot-system. Lexical bridge.

## Q. sebasma (G4574) — only 2 NT tokens

**Source:** `$WORD_STUDIES/G4574-sebasma.md`

- Acts 17:23 (plural σεβάσματα — Athenian shrines and altars) + 2 Thess 2:4 (singular σέβασμα — generic object of worship).
- Louw-Nida 53.007 — Worship, Reverence.
- Cognate with σέβομαι (G4576) "to revere, worship."
- In 2 Thess 2:4 the sg. σέβασμα with παντα is generic: "anything worshipped." Therefore NOT restricted to pagan idols but includes *any* object of religious veneration — the figure exalts himself above all of them.

## R. G868 aphistēmi (depart) — the verb cognate of apostasia

**Source:** `$WORD_STUDIES/G868-aphistemi.md`

- 15 NT verses / 6 books. Luke-Acts = 10 of 15 (67%); Pauline Pastorals + 2 Cor = 4 of 15 (27%).
- Louw-Nida 039.007 "Rebellion" — same domain as ἀποστασία G646. Cognate forms.

## S. G500 antichristos (John's vocabulary — Paul does NOT use)

**Source:** `$WORD_STUDIES/G500-antichristos.md`

- 5 NT tokens in 4 verses — all Johannine (1 John 2:18a,b, 2:22; 4:3; 2 John 7).
- Compound ἀντί (against/instead of) + Χριστός — dual force of opposition and substitution.
- **Paul never uses G500.** John never uses G459 ἄνομος or the phrase ὁ υἱὸς τῆς ἀπωλείας.
- Johannine diagnostic marker of antichristos: (a) denial that Jesus is the Christ; (b) denial of the Father-and-Son; (c) denial that Jesus Christ is come in the flesh.
- Distribution: singular `ὁ ἀντίχριστος` (1 Jn 2:18a — "shall come"; 2:22; 2 Jn 7 — a specific figure/spirit); plural `ἀντίχριστοι` (1 Jn 2:18b — "now many antichrists"); genitive `ἀντιχρίστου` (1 Jn 4:3 — "that [spirit] of antichrist").
- **The lexical bridge Paul-John is inferred**, not lexical; any identification of Paul's ὁ ἄνομος with John's ὁ ἀντίχριστος rests on thematic/structural parallel (already-now-operative; eschatological; anti-Christ-register), not on vocabulary overlap.

## T. Textual Variant hamartia vs. anomia at 2 Thess 2:3

**Source:** `$GRAMMAR_REF/greek/textual-variant-2thess-2-3.md` + `raw-data/text-comparisons.txt`

| Witness | Reading | Era / Type |
|---------|---------|-----------|
| ℵ B 0278 33 81 1739 1881 latt | τῆς **ἀνομίας** (critical text) | Alexandrian; 4th c. and earlier |
| A D Ψ 𝔐 | τῆς **ἁμαρτίας** (Byzantine / TR / KJV) | Byzantine majority |

- Both are genitive singular feminine — morphologically identical.
- Both function as attributive genitive of quality ("man [characterized] by lawlessness / by sin").
- Meaning-shift is modest: anomia is lexically tighter (opposition-to-law), hamartia broader (missing the mark). 1 Jn 3:4 equates them.
- **Either reading keeps the anomia-chain intact** — v.7 `τὸ μυστήριον τῆς ἀνομίας` and v.8 `ὁ ἄνομος` are un-varying. The v.3 variant does not dissolve the vocabulary-chain Paul builds.
