    Bible Study: The Two Genealogies of Jesus -- Contradiction or Complementary Purpose?¶

    Question¶

    Do the 2 genealogies of Jesus (Matthew 1 and Luke 3) contradict each other? Or does the context in which they are written suggest a particular meaning for each? What do Jewish culture patterns and Biblical prophecies reveal about these genealogies?

    Nave's Topics to Research (Full Entries Needed)¶

    1. GENEALOGY -- Core topic. Lists genealogies from Adam to Noah, to Abraham, to Jesus (MAT 1:1-16; LUK 3:23-38). Notes genealogy of "no spiritual significance" (MAT 3:9; 1TI 1:4; TIT 3:9). Also lists genealogy of Joseph (MAT 1; LUK 3:23-38).
    2. JESUS, THE CHRIST -- History section begins with "Genealogy of MAT 1:1-17; LUK 3:23-38." Massive entry covering entire life and ministry.
    3. DAVID -- "Genealogy of RUT 4:18-22; 1SA 16:11; 17:12; 1CH 2:3-15; MAT 1:1-6; LUK 3:31-38." Note: Jesus called "son of David" in MAT 9:27; 12:23; 15:22; 20:30,31; 21:9; 22:42; MRK 10:47,48; LUK 18:37,39. David is also a prophetic name for Christ (JER 30:9; EZK 34:23,24; 37:24,25; HOS 3:5). Prophecies about David's kingdom: 2SA 7:11-16; PSA 89:19-37; ISA 9:7; JER 23:5; LUK 1:32,33.
    4. ABRAHAM -- Covenant promises foundational to genealogy (GEN 12:1-3; 15; 17:1-22; GAL 3:6-18,29). "In thee shall all nations be blessed" (GEN 22:18; ACT 3:25; GAL 3:8).
    5. MARY -- Mother of Jesus (MAT 1:16; LUK 1:26-38). Prophecies concerning: ISA 7:14; LUK 2:35. Present at cross (JHN 19:25-27).
    6. JOSEPH -- "Husband of Mary... His genealogy MAT 1:1-16; LUK 3:23-38." Angel testifies to innocence of betrothed (MAT 1:19-24). Lives at Nazareth, belongs to Bethlehem (LUK 2:4). Three ancestors of Joseph share the name Joseph (LUK 3:24,26,30).
    7. SOLOMON -- "Son of David by Bath-sheba... An ancestor of Joseph MAT 1:6." Key figure in Matthew's line (through Solomon), absent from Luke's line (through Nathan).
    8. NATHAN -- "Son of David and Bath-sheba 2SA 5:14; 1CH 3:5; 14:4." Key figure: Nathan is David's son through whom Luke traces the genealogy, while Matthew traces through Solomon.
    9. JACOB -- "Ancestor of Jesus MAT 1:2." Also relevant: Jacob (father of Joseph) in MAT 1:16 vs Heli (father of Joseph) in LUK 3:23.
    10. JUDAH -- "The ancestor of Jesus MAT 1:2,3; REV 5:5." Tribe of Judah prophecy: GEN 49:10.
    11. ZERUBBABEL -- "Called ZOROBABEL in the genealogy of Joseph MAT 1:12; LUK 3:27." Appears in both genealogies -- key convergence point.
    12. PROPHECY -- Contains massive section: "CONCERNING THE MESSIAH, AND THE FULFILLMENT -- OLD TESTAMENT MESSIANIC PASSAGES QUOTED IN THE NEW TESTAMENT." Lists dozens of OT-NT prophecy fulfillment pairs.
    13. INHERITANCE -- "Provisions for inheritance under Levirate marriages GEN 38:7-11; NUM 36:6-9; DEU 25:5-10; RUT 3:1-8; 4:7-17." Relevant to understanding legal vs biological lineage.
    14. MATTHAT -- "Father of Heli, and ancestor of Joseph LUK 3:24." Important for understanding Joseph's father in Luke's genealogy.
    15. MARRIAGE -- "Levirate (the brother required to marry a brother's widow) GEN 38:8,11; DEU 25:5-10; RUT 4:5; MAT 22:24." Relevant to legal inheritance and how a man could have two "fathers."

    Key Verse Passages to Retrieve¶

    The Two Genealogies (Complete Text Required)¶

    • Matthew 1:1-17 -- Matthew's genealogy (Abraham to Jesus, descending order)
    • Luke 3:23-38 -- Luke's genealogy (Jesus to Adam/God, ascending order)

    Context Surrounding Each Genealogy¶

    • Matthew 1:18-25 -- What immediately follows Matthew's genealogy (virgin birth narrative)
    • Luke 3:21-22 -- What immediately precedes Luke's genealogy (Jesus' baptism)
    • Luke 4:1-13 -- What immediately follows Luke's genealogy (temptation)

    Davidic Covenant and Lineage Prophecies¶

    • 2 Samuel 7:11-16 -- God's covenant with David about his throne
    • 1 Chronicles 17:11-14 -- Parallel Davidic covenant passage
    • Psalm 89:3,4 -- God's covenant with David
    • Psalm 89:19-37 -- Extended Davidic covenant promises
    • Psalm 132:11 -- "The LORD hath sworn in truth unto David"
    • Isaiah 9:6,7 -- Messiah on David's throne
    • Isaiah 11:1,10 -- Branch from Jesse's root
    • Jeremiah 23:5,6 -- Righteous Branch of David
    • Jeremiah 33:15-26 -- David's line never cut off
    • Ezekiel 34:23,24 -- "My servant David" as shepherd
    • Ezekiel 37:24,25 -- David their king forever
    • Hosea 3:5 -- Return and seek "David their king"

    Abrahamic Covenant Promises¶

    • Genesis 12:1-3 -- Abrahamic covenant ("in thee shall all families be blessed")
    • Genesis 22:18 -- "In thy seed shall all the nations of the earth be blessed"
    • Genesis 49:10 -- "The sceptre shall not depart from Judah"
    • Galatians 3:16 -- "To Abraham and his seed... which is Christ"

    Messianic Lineage Prophecies¶

    • Genesis 3:15 -- "Seed of the woman" (protoevangelium)
    • Numbers 24:17 -- "A Star out of Jacob, a Sceptre out of Israel"
    • Isaiah 7:14 -- Virgin shall conceive (connects to genealogy purpose)
    • Micah 5:2 -- Ruler from Bethlehem
    • Romans 1:1-3 -- "Made of the seed of David according to the flesh"
    • Acts 2:29,30 -- Peter on David and the promise to his seed
    • Acts 13:22,23 -- "Of this man's seed hath God... raised unto Israel a Saviour"
    • Revelation 22:16 -- "I am the root and the offspring of David"
    • Revelation 5:5 -- "Lion of the tribe of Judah, the Root of David"

    David's Sons: Solomon and Nathan¶

    • 2 Samuel 5:14 -- Nathan, son of David
    • 1 Chronicles 3:5 -- Nathan, son of David and Bath-sheba
    • 2 Samuel 12:24,25 -- Solomon, son of David and Bath-sheba
    • Matthew 1:6 -- Matthew traces through Solomon
    • Luke 3:31 -- Luke traces through Nathan

    Joseph's Father: Jacob vs Heli¶

    • Matthew 1:16 -- "Jacob begat Joseph the husband of Mary"
    • Luke 3:23 -- "Joseph, which was the son of Heli"
    • Luke 3:24 -- Matthat, father of Heli

    Zerubbabel -- Convergence Point¶

    • Matthew 1:12 -- "Jechonias begat Salathiel; and Salathiel begat Zorobabel"
    • Luke 3:27 -- "Zorobabel, which was the son of Salathiel"
    • 1 Chronicles 3:17-19 -- OT genealogy of Zerubbabel

    Jeconiah Curse¶

    • Jeremiah 22:24-30 -- Curse on Coniah/Jeconiah ("no man of his seed shall prosper, sitting upon the throne of David")
    • Matthew 1:11 -- Josias begat Jechonias (Jeconiah in Matthew's line)

    Women in Matthew's Genealogy (Unusual Inclusion)¶

    • Matthew 1:3 -- Tamar (GEN 38:12-26)
    • Matthew 1:5 -- Rahab (JOS 2:1-21; 6:25)
    • Matthew 1:5 -- Ruth (RUT 1; 4:13-17)
    • Matthew 1:6 -- "Her that had been the wife of Urias" (Bath-sheba; 2SA 11:2-5)
    • Matthew 1:16 -- Mary

    Jesus Called "Son of David"¶

    • Matthew 9:27 -- Two blind men cry "Son of David"
    • Matthew 12:23 -- "Is not this the son of David?"
    • Matthew 15:22 -- Canaanite woman: "O Lord, thou Son of David"
    • Matthew 20:30,31 -- "Have mercy on us, O Lord, thou Son of David"
    • Matthew 21:9 -- "Hosanna to the Son of David"
    • Matthew 22:41-46 -- Jesus' question: "Whose son is the Messiah?"
    • Mark 10:47,48 -- Bartimaeus: "Jesus, thou Son of David"
    • Mark 12:35-37 -- "How say the scribes that Christ is the Son of David?"
    • Luke 1:32,33 -- Angel to Mary: "the throne of his father David"
    • Luke 18:37,39 -- "Jesus of Nazareth... Son of David"

    Genealogy of No Spiritual Significance (Caution Verses)¶

    • Matthew 3:9 -- "Think not to say, We have Abraham to our father"
    • 1 Timothy 1:4 -- "Neither give heed to fables and endless genealogies"
    • Titus 3:9 -- "Avoid foolish questions, and genealogies"

    OT Genealogy Parallels¶

    • Ruth 4:18-22 -- Genealogy from Pharez to David
    • 1 Chronicles 2:3-15 -- Genealogy of David
    • 1 Chronicles 3:1-24 -- David's descendants
    • Genesis 5 -- Adam to Noah genealogy (pattern for Luke)
    • Genesis 11:10-32 -- Shem to Abraham genealogy

    Inheritance and Levirate Marriage Laws¶

    • Numbers 27:6-11 -- Daughters may inherit when no sons
    • Numbers 36:6-9 -- Inheritance within tribes through marriage
    • Deuteronomy 25:5-10 -- Levirate marriage law
    • Ruth 3:1-8; 4:7-17 -- Levirate marriage example (in Jesus' lineage)

    Virgin Birth Context (Connects to Genealogy Purpose)¶

    • Luke 1:26-38 -- Annunciation to Mary
    • Luke 1:27 -- Mary betrothed to Joseph "of the house of David"
    • Matthew 1:18-25 -- Joseph's response to Mary's pregnancy
    • Galatians 4:4 -- "Made of a woman, made under the law"

It also discusses Gen 3:15 "her seed" as pointing to the virgin birth, and how "the faithful line" was preserved through unexpected means. This directly connects to the genealogy question.

    Focus Areas¶

    1. Matthew's genealogy: context, structure, audience, purpose
    2. Written to a Jewish audience (Gospel of Matthew)
    3. Descending order: Abraham to Jesus
    4. Structured in three groups of 14 generations (MAT 1:17)
    5. Traces through the royal line (Solomon)
    6. Includes four unusual women (Tamar, Rahab, Ruth, Bathsheba)

    7. Establishes Jesus as legal heir to David's throne through Joseph

    8. Luke's genealogy: context, structure, audience, purpose

    9. Written to Theophilus/Gentile audience (Gospel of Luke)
    10. Ascending order: Jesus back to Adam, then to God
    11. Placed after baptism ("Thou art my beloved Son") and before temptation
    12. Traces through Nathan (son of David) instead of Solomon
    13. Goes all the way back to Adam -- universal scope

    14. Possibly traces Mary's biological line

    15. Points of divergence between the two lists

    16. Joseph's father: Jacob (MAT 1:16) vs Heli (LUK 3:23)
    17. David to Joseph: through Solomon (Matthew) vs through Nathan (Luke)
    18. Direction: descending (Matthew) vs ascending (Luke)
    19. Scope: from Abraham (Matthew) vs all the way to Adam/God (Luke)

    20. Zerubbabel/Salathiel appear in both but surrounding names differ

    21. OT prophecies about Messiah's lineage (Davidic, Abrahamic)

    22. Seed of woman (GEN 3:15)
    23. Through Abraham (GEN 12:3; 22:18)
    24. Through Judah (GEN 49:10)
    25. Through David (2SA 7:12-16; PSA 89:3,4; ISA 9:7; JER 23:5)
    26. From Bethlehem (MIC 5:2)
    27. Star out of Jacob (NUM 24:17)

    28. The Jeconiah curse problem (JER 22:24-30) and how the two genealogies resolve it

    29. Jewish cultural practices around genealogy, legal vs biological descent

    30. Levirate marriage (DEU 25:5-10) -- a man could have both a legal father and biological father
    31. Legal adoption and inheritance rights through marriage
    32. Daughters' inheritance rights (NUM 27:6-11; 36:6-9)
    33. The significance of tribal records and genealogical enrollment (NUM 1:18; NEH 7:5)

    34. How a son-in-law could be listed as a "son" in Jewish reckoning

    35. How the two genealogies together fulfill prophecy

    36. Matthew establishes legal/royal right to David's throne (through Joseph)
    37. Luke may establish biological descent from David (through Mary)
    38. Together they solve the Jeconiah curse: legal right through Joseph (Matthew), but biological descent bypasses the curse through Nathan's line (Luke)

    39. Both confirm descent from David, Abraham, and Judah

    40. The role of women in Matthew's genealogy (unusual for Jewish genealogies)

    41. Tamar (GEN 38) -- Gentile connection, irregular circumstances
    42. Rahab (JOS 2) -- Canaanite prostitute who had faith
    43. Ruth (RUT 1-4) -- Moabitess, covenant faithfulness
    44. Bathsheba ("her that had been the wife of Urias") -- Hittite connection
    45. Mary -- virgin, Holy Spirit conception
    46. Pattern: God's grace working through unexpected, often non-Jewish women
    47. Foreshadows the gospel going to Gentiles
