    Topics Research: Did Jephthah Sacrifice His Daughter, or Dedicate Her to Perpetual Virginity?¶

    Question¶

    Did Jephthah actually sacrifice his daughter as a burnt offering (Judges 11:29-40), or did he dedicate her to perpetual virginity / lifelong temple service? What does the Hebrew text, the grammar of the vow, and the surrounding biblical theology indicate?

    Topics (Nave's Topical Dictionary — full entries organized by theme)¶

    A. The Narrative of Jephthah's Vow¶

    JEPHTHAH¶

    (A judge — leader, hero — of Israel) - Illegitimate, not entitled to inherit father's property: Jdg 11:1, 2 - Escapes half-brothers' violence, lives in Tob: Jdg 11:3 - Recalled from Tob by elders of Gilead: Jdg 11:5 - Made captain of the army: Jdg 11:5-11 - Made head of Gilead: Jdg 11:7-11 - Diplomatic message to king of Ammon (Torah-literate argument): Jdg 11:12-28 - Leads Israel against Ammon (Spirit of YHWH upon him): Jdg 11:29-33 - His rash vow concerning his daughter: Jdg 11:31, 34-40 - Falsely accused by Ephraimites: Jdg 12:1 - Leads Gileadites against Ephraim (Shibboleth): Jdg 12:4 - Judges Israel six years, dies, buried in Gilead: Jdg 12:7 - Faith of: Heb 11:32

    Nave's editorial note: Nave classifies Jdg 11:31, 34-40 explicitly under "rash vow" — this is an interpretive choice consistent with the literal-sacrifice reading, not a Scriptural verdict.

    B. Vow Ethics, Legal Frameworks, and Parallels¶

    VOWS¶

    Mosaic laws concerning: Lev 23:37, 38; Num 29:39 Must be voluntary (see below); must be performed (see below) Redemption price of vowed persons (priest's valuation by age and sex): Lev 27:1-13 ⬅ key for Jephthah Redemption of real estate: Lev 27:14, 15, 16-25 Edible things offered in vows (same-day consumption): Lev 7:16-18 Vows to be brought to tabernacle/temple: Deut 12:6, 11, 17, 18, 26 Belonged to priests: Num 18:14

    RASH VOWS: - By Jephthah (consecration of daughter as sacrifice): Jdg 11:29-40 ⬅ study-central - Israelites vs. Benjamites: Jdg 21:5, 6; with 20:21

    Things forbidden in vows: whore/dog hire (Deut 23:18) A minor's vow of himself (Corban abuse): Mrk 7:11-13

    Unclassified vow texts: Gen 28:20-21; 31:13; Lev 5:4-13 ⬅ rash-oath atonement pathway; Lev 22:18-25; Num 15:2-16; Num 30:1-16 ⬅ annulment law; Deut 23:18, 21-23; Job 22:27; Ps 22:25; 50:14-15; 56:12; 61:5, 8; 65:1; 66:13-14; 76:11; 116:14-19; Prov 20:25; Eccl 5:4-6 ⬅ "error" off-ramp; Jonah 2:9; Nahum 1:15.

    INSTANCES OF: - Jacob: Gen 28:20-22 (structural vow parallel) - Micah's mother (silver for idol): Jdg 17:2-3 - Hannah (Samuel): 1 Sam 1:11, with 1:27-28 ⬅ controlling parallel for lifelong-dedication - Elkanah: 1 Sam 1:21 - Absalom: 2 Sam 15:7-8 - Job (no fornication-thoughts): Job 31:1 - David: Ps 132:2 - Ananias/Sapphira: Acts 5:1-11 - Jews vs. Paul (rash oath): Acts 23:12-15

    Cross-reference: CONTRACT, COVENANT, NAZARITE.

    C. Dedication and Consecration (the Dedication-Reading Register)¶

    DEDICATION¶

    • Law concerning dedicated things: Lev 27; Num 18:14; 1 Chr 26:26-27
    • Must be without blemish: Lev 22:18-23; Mal 1:14
    • Not redeemable: Lev 27:28, 29 ⬅ but note: this is the CHEREM category (Lev 27:28-29), distinct from the ordinary NEDER category of Lev 27:1-8
    • Offering must be voluntary: Lev 1:3; 22:19
    • Tabernacle: Num 7
    • Solomon's temple: 1 Kgs 8; 2 Chr 7:5
    • Second temple: Ezr 6:16-17
    • Wall of Jerusalem: Neh 12:27-43
    • Houses: Deut 20:5
    • Of Samuel by his mother: 1 Sam 1:11, 22 ⬅ lifelong-dedication paradigm
    • Feast of Dedication: Jhn 10:22; 1 Kgs 8:65; 2 Chr 7:8-10

    Cross-refs: CONSECRATION, OFFERINGS, VOWS, FEAST OF DEDICATION.

    CONSECRATION¶

    Personal: Ps 51:17; Mat 13:44-46; Rom 6:13, 16, 19; 12:1; 2 Cor 8:5 Conditional: Gen 28:20-22; 2 Sam 15:7-8 (see DEDICATION, OFFERINGS) INSTANCES OF: - Cain and Abel: Gen 4:4-7 - Abraham of Isaac: Gen 22:9-12 ⬅ Akedah — arrested sacrifice pattern - Jephthah of his daughter: Jdg 11:30, 31, 34-40 ⬅ grouped under consecration-dedication, not sacrifice - Hannah of Samuel: 1 Sam 1:11, 24-28 ⬅ lifelong service - David consecrates water: 2 Sam 23:16 - Zichri of himself: 2 Chr 17:16

    Editorial note: Nave places Jephthah's daughter under CONSECRATION (not SACRIFICE), grouping her with Isaac (arrested) and Samuel (living-served). The semantic grouping supports the dedication-reading at the editorial level.

    D. The Virgin / Virginity Register¶

    VIRGIN¶

    • Proofs of: Deut 22:13-21
    • Dowry of: Exo 22:17
    • Character of, protected: Deut 22:17-21, 23-24
    • Betrothal as quasi-marriage: Deut 22:23-24
    • Distinguishing apparel: 2 Sam 13:18
    • Priest could marry only a virgin: Lev 21:14
    • Mourn in the temple: Lam 1:4; 2:10
    • Virginity of, bewailed: Jdg 11:37-39 ⬅ study-central
    • Parable of wise/foolish: Mat 25:1-13
    • Mother of Jesus was a virgin: Isa 7:14; Mat 1:23; Luk 1:27
    • Paul's counsel re virgins: 1 Cor 7

    FIGURATIVE: - Body of Christ (ekklesia): Isa 62:5; Jer 14:17; 31:4, 13; 2 Cor 11:2 - Personal purity: 1 Cor 7:25, 37; Rev 14:4

    Note: Nave's heading "Virginity of, bewailed Jdg 11:37-39" classifies the daughter's episode under the LIVING-virgin-mourning-a-condition cluster (parallel to Lam 1:4; 2:10 — virgins mourning in the temple; living women), not under a funeral-for-the-dead cluster.

    E. The Nazarite Register (Lifelong Living Dedication)¶

    NAZARITE¶

    • Law concerning: Num 6:1-21; Jdg 13:5
    • Character of: Lam 4:7; Amos 2:11-12

    INSTANCES: - Samson: Jdg 13:5, 7; 16:17 - Samuel: 1 Sam 1:11 ⬅ same verse as Hannah's vow — the dedication parallel - Rechabites: Jer 35 - John the Baptist: Mat 11:18; Luk 1:15; 7:33

    Note: All Nazarite instances are LIVING consecration-by-separation. Samuel's Nazarite-like dedication (explicit "no razor" in 1 Sam 1:11) is the controlling parallel for reading Jephthah's vow as potentially dedication-style.

    F. The Oath Register¶

    OATH¶

    (A solemn qualification) — used in solemnizing covenants between persons and in self-imprecation to God.

    Key instances for this study: - Elders of Gilead swear pledge to Jephthah: Jdg 11:10 - Israelites swear at Mizpeh (Benjamin rash oath): Jdg 21:5 - Saul's rash oath overridden by people for Jonathan: 1 Sam 14:24-45 - Ecc 5:4-6 (Qohelet's oath-ethic — "an error" off-ramp)

    NT oath-ethics (Jesus + James): - Mat 5:33-37 (swear not at all) - Jas 5:12 (yea, yea; nay, nay)

    Attributed to God: Gen 22:16 (Akedah oath-of-promise); Heb 6:13-18; 7:21, 28; Rev 10:6.

    Unclassified: Exo 20:7; 22:10-11; Lev 6:2-5; 19:12; Num 5:19-24; Deut 5:11; 6:13; 10:20; 1 Kgs 8:31-32; Ps 15:1-4; Ecc 8:2; Jer 4:2; 5:2, 7; 7:8-9; 12:16; Dan 9:11; 12:7; Hos 4:15; Mat 23:18-22; 26:63; Mrk 6:26; Mrk 14:71; Acts 23:12-14; 2 Cor 1:23; Gal 1:20; Heb 6:16; Jas 5:12; Rev 10:5-6.

    G. Human Sacrifice (Categorically Prohibited)¶

    MOLECH (Also MOLOCH, MILCOM — Idol of Ammon)¶

    • An idol of the Ammonites: Acts 7:43 (parallel 1 Kgs 11:5 "Milcom the abomination of the Ammonites")
    • Worshiped by Solomon's wives, and by Solomon: 1 Kgs 11:1-8
    • Children sacrificed to Molech — the entire prohibition-and-condemnation corpus:
    • 2 Kgs 23:10 (Josiah defiles Topheth)
    • Jer 32:35 (Hinnom sacrifices condemned)
    • 2 Kgs 16:3 (Ahaz "made his son to pass through the fire")
    • 2 Kgs 21:6 (Manasseh)
    • 2 Chr 28:3 (Ahaz — parallel)
    • Isa 57:5 (slaying children in valleys)
    • Jer 7:31 ⬅ divine disavowal: "which I commanded them not, neither came it into my heart"
    • Ezek 16:20-21 ("slain my children")
    • Ezek 20:26, 31 ("pass through fire")
    • Ezek 23:37, 39 (sacrificed "to be devoured")
    • See Lev 18:21; 20:2-5 (Pentateuchal prohibition — death penalty for giving seed to Molech).

    Critical observation: Molech was the idol of Ammon specifically — the very nation Jephthah was fighting (Jdg 11). Under the literal-sacrifice reading, Jephthah would be reproducing the exact abomination of the enemy god immediately after defeating them — a theological grotesquerie that deepens rather than solves the problem.

    HUMAN SACRIFICE → OFFERING, HUMAN¶

    (Nave's entry redirects; see MOLECH prohibition-condemnation cluster above plus Gen 22 Akedah and Jdg 11:29-40 Jephthah as contested case.)

    BURNT OFFERING → OFFERINGS, BURNT¶

    (The olah technical category; normally animal — bullock, ram, lamb, kid, bird; Lev 1:3-17; Num 28-29 daily/festival schedule. Human immolation as burnt-offering appears in Scripture only at: Gen 22 Akedah — ARRESTED; 2 Kgs 3:27 — Mesha the Moabite king, contrary to YHWH, producing "great indignation upon Israel"; and as the prophets' catalog of condemned Molech-worship. The literal reading of Jdg 11:39 would place Jephthah in a category occupied only by pagan enemies and by the Akedah-test — uniquely applied to a voluntary Israelite daughter.)
