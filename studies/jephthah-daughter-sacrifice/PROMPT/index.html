    Bible Study: Did Jephthah Actually Sacrifice His Daughter, or Dedicate Her to Perpetual Virginity?¶

    Question¶

    Did Jephthah actually sacrifice his daughter as a burnt offering (Judges 11:29-40), or did he dedicate her to perpetual virginity / lifelong temple service? What does the Hebrew text, the grammar of the vow, and the surrounding biblical theology indicate?

    Prior Research Summary¶

    v1 prior iteration (jephthah-daughter-sacrifice-v1, 2026-04-16) concluded the weight of biblical evidence modestly favors the perpetual-virginity / lifelong-dedication reading over literal burnt-offering. v1's five main legs:

    1. Narrator's lexical restraint at the climax (Jdg 11:39) — colorless wayyaas lah et-nidroavoids every available sacrifice verb (zabach,shachat,saraph,alahHiphil,heevir baesh) — contrast 2 Kgs 3:27, 16:3, 21:6, 23:10, Jer 19:5, Ezek 23:37-39, Gen 22:10.
    2. lo-yadah ish` (11:39) as WX-QATAL stative/durative — every other canonical occurrence describes living women's chastity (Gen 24:16; Num 31:17-18, 35; Jdg 21:11-12; 1 Kgs 1:4).
    3. bethulim (H1331) mourning of virginity-state, not impending death — no OT parallel for anticipatory 2-month self-mourning (cf. Gen 50:10 Jacob 7d; Num 20:29 Aaron 30d).
    4. letannot (H8567) Piel in Jdg 11:40 — dis legomenon; Jdg 5:11 control = "recount/celebrate" (yetannu tzidqot YHWH).
    5. Torah's categorical prohibition + Jer 7:31 divine disavowal forecloses divinely-accepted sacrifice.

    v1 conceded default syntax of Jdg 11:31 (wehayah laYHWH wehaalitihu olah) is conjunctive weqatal;alahHiphil +olahis a technical cultic collocation (Gen 8:20; 22:13); Jephthah'slo ukhal lashuv` (11:35) indicates he understood the vow as irrevocable.

    v1 gaps to close in v2: (a) Hebrew-grammar argument lacked canonical grammar-reference entries on WX-QATAL disjunctive/circumstantial and weqatal-apodosis; (b) LXX cross-check on 11:31, 39, 40 not done systematically; (c) Targum Jonathan/Phinehas-redemption gloss not addressed; (d) letannot vs Jdg 5:11 LXX verb-choice not tested; (e) Jephthah Torah-literacy argument (Jdg 11:12-28 vs Num 20-21) not surfaced; (f) Heb 11:32-34 Greek aorist verbs not parsed and matched to specific Jephthah acts.

    External corpus signal (from 00-references.md): - EGW silent on the pericope — doctrine rests on Scripture alone (principle_scripture_authority). - Clarke Commentary (28187, 28216, 28230, 28231, 28288): disjunctive-waw vow reading; moral-impossibility argument; Spirit-of-YHWH argument; Targum Jonathan gloss on 11:39 — "Jephthah did not consult Phinehas... who would have redeemed her with money" (literal-sacrifice view but Torah-violation framing). - Henry Commentary (9409, 9411, 9412, 9414, 9976): surveys both; objects "no law or custom intimated celibacy as religion"; notes Num 30:5 father's annulment; connects 1 Sam 1:11 Hannah/Samuel as controlling parallel. - Edersheim BHOTV3 140.1, 141.1: literal sacrifice; "praise" (not "lament") for letannot; rejects the "rough-times Torah-ignorant Jephthah" defense since Jdg 11:15-27 demonstrates Pentateuchal literacy. - Josephus Ant. 5.7.6, 5.7.10: literal sacrifice; "neither conformable to the law nor acceptable to God"; paraphrases vow as "what living creature soever" (supports disjunctive-vow reading that vow anticipated any creature, not specifically a human). - SU silent; 1 Meqabyan 4:30 — general Satanic condemnation of child sacrifice (minor lead, skip). - Sirach 18:22 — intertestamental vow-ethic pushed vow-payment absolutism (contextualizes Josephus' literal reading).

    Focus Areas¶

    1. Grammatical spine of Jdg 11:30-31 — default-conjunctive vs. disjunctive waw.
    2. WHAT: Determine whether wehayah laYHWH wehaalitihu olah` grammatically permits, favors, or forbids a disjunctive reading ("shall be to YHWH OR I will offer up as burnt offering").
    3. WHY: This is THE hinge. Default weqatal-chain is conjunctive (v1 conceded). Clarke/Henry argue disjunctive is admissible given waw's polyfunctionality and the unique vow-context.

    4. HOW: READ $GRAMMAR_REF/hebrew/syntax-conditional-vow.md, $GRAMMAR_REF/hebrew/syntax-waw-conjunction.md, $GRAMMAR_REF/hebrew/weqatal-apodosis-vow.md, $GRAMMAR_REF/passages/jdg-11-30-31.md. Parse all OT wehayah + X + we-QATAL vow/conditional constructions: Gen 28:20-22 (Jacob), Num 21:2 (Hormah), 1 Sam 1:11 (Hannah), 2 Sam 15:7-8 (Absalom). Tabulate whether any unambiguously carries disjunctive force. Scrutinize v1's Exo 21:15 citation for noun-level vs clausal disjunction. Run semantic_grammar.py "disjunctive waw" --hebrew and semantic_grammar.py "weqatal apodosis vow" --hebrew.

    5. Lexical restraint in Jdg 11:39 — what the narrator didn't say.

    6. WHAT: Establish that the narrator in 11:39 (wayyaas lah et-nidro asher nadar`) systematically avoids every Hebrew sacrifice-verb available to him.
    7. WHY: v1 leg 1 is strongest; in every parallel reported-killing (2 Kgs 3:27 Mesha wayyaalehu olah`; 2 Kgs 16:3; 21:6; 23:10; Jer 19:5; Gen 22:10), the Hebrew narrator names the act. Silence here is diagnostic.

    8. HOW: READ library entries for H5927 alah, H5930olah, H2076 zabach, H2763 charam. Run search_strongs.py --verses H5927 "offered" and --verses H2076 to confirm these verbs are available in narrative-reporting mode. Retrieve full text of 2 Kgs 3:27; 2 Kgs 16:3; 21:6; 23:10; Jer 7:31; 19:5; Ezek 16:20-21; 23:37-39; Ps 106:37-38; Gen 22:10. Tabulate the sacrifice-verb in each — contrast with 11:39's wayyaas`.

    9. lo-yadah ish` as WX-QATAL circumstantial — durative chastity clause.

    10. WHAT: Determine whether the subject-fronted we-hi lo yadah ish` is (a) summary of a completed event (she died virgin) or (b) durative/circumstantial clause describing an ongoing state.
    11. WHY: v1 leg 2. WX-QATAL after wayyiqtol is typically circumstantial/stative; every OT parallel construction describes LIVING women's virginal state.
