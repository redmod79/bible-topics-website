    Verses: Did Jephthah Sacrifice His Daughter, or Dedicate Her to Perpetual Virginity?¶

    All verse text in KJV (source: D:/bible/tools/data/kjv.txt). Hebrew/Greek parsing from hebrew_parser.py and greek_parser.py (ETCBC/BHSA; N1904). Raw parsing in raw-data/jdg-11-hebrew-parse.txt and raw-data/heb-11-greek-parse.txt.

    A. Primary Narrative — Judges 11 (FULL CHAPTER)¶

    Judges 11:1-11 (Jephthah's Origin and Appointment)¶

    Jdg 11:1 Now Jephthah the Gileadite was a mighty man of valour, and he [was] the son of an harlot: and Gilead begat Jephthah. Jdg 11:2 And Gilead's wife bare him sons; and his wife's sons grew up, and they thrust out Jephthah, and said unto him, Thou shalt not inherit in our father's house; for thou [art] the son of a strange woman. Jdg 11:3 Then Jephthah fled from his brethren, and dwelt in the land of Tob: and there were gathered vain men to Jephthah, and went out with him. Jdg 11:4 And it came to pass in process of time, that the children of Ammon made war against Israel. Jdg 11:5 And it was so, that when the children of Ammon made war against Israel, the elders of Gilead went to fetch Jephthah out of the land of Tob: Jdg 11:6 And they said unto Jephthah, Come, and be our captain, that we may fight with the children of Ammon. Jdg 11:7 And Jephthah said unto the elders of Gilead, Did not ye hate me, and expel me out of my father's house? and why are ye come unto me now when ye are in distress? Jdg 11:8 And the elders of Gilead said unto Jephthah, Therefore we turn again to thee now, that thou mayest go with us, and fight against the children of Ammon, and be our head over all the inhabitants of Gilead. Jdg 11:9 And Jephthah said unto the elders of Gilead, If ye bring me home again to fight against the children of Ammon, and the LORD deliver them before me, shall I be your head? Jdg 11:10 And the elders of Gilead said unto Jephthah, The LORD be witness between us, if we do not so according to thy words. Jdg 11:11 Then Jephthah went with the elders of Gilead, and the people made him head and captain over them: and Jephthah uttered all his words before the LORD in Mizpeh.

    Judges 11:12-28 (Diplomatic Speech to Ammon — Torah-Literacy Test)¶

    Jdg 11:12 And Jephthah sent messengers unto the king of the children of Ammon, saying, What hast thou to do with me, that thou art come against me to fight in my land? Jdg 11:13 And the king of the children of Ammon answered unto the messengers of Jephthah, Because Israel took away my land, when they came up out of Egypt, from Arnon even unto Jabbok, and unto Jordan: now therefore restore those [lands] again peaceably. Jdg 11:14 And Jephthah sent messengers again unto the king of the children of Ammon: Jdg 11:15 And said unto him, Thus saith Jephthah, Israel took not away the land of Moab, nor the land of the children of Ammon: Jdg 11:16 But when Israel came up from Egypt, and walked through the wilderness unto the Red sea, and came to Kadesh; Jdg 11:17 Then Israel sent messengers unto the king of Edom, saying, Let me, I pray thee, pass through thy land: but the king of Edom would not hearken [thereto]. And in like manner they sent unto the king of Moab: but he would not [consent]: and Israel abode in Kadesh. Jdg 11:18 Then they went along through the wilderness, and compassed the land of Edom, and the land of Moab, and came by the east side of the land of Moab, and pitched on the other side of Arnon, but came not within the border of Moab: for Arnon [was] the border of Moab. Jdg 11:19 And Israel sent messengers unto Sihon king of the Amorites, the king of Heshbon; and Israel said unto him, Let us pass, we pray thee, through thy land into my place. Jdg 11:20 But Sihon trusted not Israel to pass through his coast: but Sihon gathered all his people together, and pitched in Jahaz, and fought against Israel. Jdg 11:21 And the LORD God of Israel delivered Sihon and all his people into the hand of Israel, and they smote them: so Israel possessed all the land of the Amorites, the inhabitants of that country. Jdg 11:22 And they possessed all the coasts of the Amorites, from Arnon even unto Jabbok, and from the wilderness even unto Jordan. Jdg 11:23 So now the LORD God of Israel hath dispossessed the Amorites from before his people Israel, and shouldest thou possess it? Jdg 11:24 Wilt not thou possess that which Chemosh thy god giveth thee to possess? So whomsoever the LORD our God shall drive out from before us, them will we possess. Jdg 11:25 And now [art] thou any thing better than Balak the son of Zippor, king of Moab? did he ever strive against Israel, or did he ever fight against them, Jdg 11:26 While Israel dwelt in Heshbon and her towns, and in Aroer and her towns, and in all the cities that [be] along by the coasts of Arnon, three hundred years? why therefore did ye not recover [them] within that time? Jdg 11:27 Wherefore I have not sinned against thee, but thou doest me wrong to war against me: the LORD the Judge be judge this day between the children of Israel and the children of Ammon. Jdg 11:28 Howbeit the king of the children of Ammon hearkened not unto the words of Jephthah which he sent him.

    Torah-literacy observation: Jephthah's speech (vv. 15-27) closely tracks Num 20:14-21; 21:13-35; and Deut 2:4-9, 19 — the Exodus-era narrative of passage through Edom/Moab, Arnon boundary, conquest of Sihon. Jephthah demonstrates accurate knowledge of Pentateuchal history — which is load-bearing for the analysis: the "he was an ignorant outlaw" defense of the literal reading collapses (Edersheim BHOTV3 141.1). A Torah-literate Jephthah knows Lev 18:21; 20:2-5; Deut 12:31; 18:10 and knows Lev 27:1-8 / Num 30.

    Judges 11:29-31 (Spirit + Vow — CENTRAL)¶

    Jdg 11:29 Then the Spirit of the LORD came upon Jephthah, and he passed over Gilead, and Manasseh, and passed over Mizpeh of Gilead, and from Mizpeh of Gilead he passed over [unto] the children of Ammon. Jdg 11:30 And Jephthah vowed a vow unto the LORD, and said, If thou shalt without fail deliver the children of Ammon into mine hands, Jdg 11:31 Then it shall be, that whatsoever cometh forth of the doors of my house to meet me, when I return in peace from the children of Ammon, shall surely be the LORD'S, and I will offer it up for a burnt offering.

    Hebrew parsing of v. 30 (key elements): - וַיִּדַּר (Qal.Wayq.3ms נדר) "and he vowed" + cognate accusative נֶדֶר — solemn vow-formula - אִם (Conj "if") + נָתוֹן תִּתֵּן (inf.abs.+Impf.2ms נתן) — emphatic protasis "if thou wilt indeed deliver"

    Hebrew parsing of v. 31 (CENTRAL): - וְהָיָה (Qal.Perf.3ms weqatal) — apodosis head - הַיּוֹצֵא אֲשֶׁר יֵצֵא (Qal.Ptcp.ms + relative + Impf.3ms יצא) — casus pendens + cognate-verb relative; maximally indefinite referent - לִקְרָאתִי (לקרא Qal.InfCon+1s) "to meet me" — social-encounter idiom skewing human - וְהָיָה לַיהוָה (weqatal + dative) — "and shall be to YHWH" (plain dative; NOT Hannah's natan+kol-yemei-chayyav) - וְהַעֲלִיתִהוּ עֹלָה (Verb.Hiphil.Perf.1s+3ms עלה "cause to go up" + Noun.fs.Abs עולה "burnt-offering") — technical cultic collocation (Hiphil alah + olah) used at Gen 8:20; 22:2, 13; Lev 17:8; 2 Kgs 3:27

    Grammar-reference entries: - grammar-reference/passages/jdg-11-30-31.md — full analysis - grammar-reference/hebrew/syntax-conditional-vow.md — im + impf. protasis / weqatal apodosis template - grammar-reference/hebrew/syntax-waw-conjunction.md — waw conjunctive/disjunctive debate - grammar-reference/hebrew/weqatal-apodosis-vow.md — weqatal chain in vow apodoses

    The disjunctive-waw question (v. 31): The third weqatal וְהַעֲלִיתִהוּ can grammatically be read disjunctively ("OR I will offer it up") per W-O'C §39.2 polyfunctionality of waw + legal-enumeration parallels (Exo 21:15 — "his father OR his mother"). But default force of a weqatal chain is conjunctive ("and"). Hebrew has a dedicated disjunctive particle אוֹ (used in v. 34 "neither son אוֹ or daughter"); v. 31 does NOT use אוֹ. LXX A-text uses καί (conjunctive); B-text uses asyndeton. Neither LXX recension uses ἤ. Grammar admits the disjunctive reading as MARKED; the unmarked default is conjunctive.

    Judges 11:32-33 (Victory)¶

    Jdg 11:32 So Jephthah passed over unto the children of Ammon to fight against them; and the LORD delivered them into his hands. Jdg 11:33 And he smote them from Aroer, even till thou come to Minnith, [even] twenty cities, and unto the plain of the vineyards, with a very great slaughter. Thus the children of Ammon were subdued before the children of Israel.

    Judges 11:34 (Daughter Emerges — yechidah Akedah Echo)¶

    Jdg 11:34 And Jephthah came to Mizpeh unto his house, and, behold, his daughter came out to meet him with timbrels and with dances: and she [was his] only child; beside her he had neither son nor daughter.

    Hebrew parsing (central): - יֹצֵאת (Qal.Ptcp.fs.Abs יצא) "going out" — feminine counterpart to v. 31 הַיּוֹצֵא - לִקְרָאתוֹ (לקרא Qal.InfCon+3ms) "to meet him" — social-encounter idiom - בְתֻפִּים וּבִמְחֹלוֹת — "with timbrels and dances" (victory-welcome cultural marker) - וְרַק הִיא יְחִידָה — "and only she [was] an only-one" (Adj.fs.Abs H3173 יָחִיד) — direct lexical echo of Gen 22:2 אֶת־בִּנְךָ אֶת־יְחִידְךָ ("thy son, thine only son") - אֵין לוֹ מִמֶּנּוּ בֵּן אוֹ־בַת — "he had from him neither son OR daughter" — note the explicit use of disjunctive particle אוֹ here (contrast with v. 31 where the waw, not אוֹ, was used; the author plainly had אוֹ in his idiolect but did not reach for it in v. 31)

    Judges 11:35 (Jephthah's Lament)¶

    Jdg 11:35 And it came to pass, when he saw her, that he rent his clothes, and said, Alas, my daughter! thou hast brought me very low, and thou art one of them that trouble me: for I have opened my mouth unto the LORD, and I cannot go back.

    Hebrew parsing: - הַכְרֵעַ הִכְרַעְתִּנִי (Hiphil.Inf.Abs + Hiphil.Perf.2fs+1s כרע) — tautological inf.abs. "thou hast utterly bowed me down" - פָּצִיתִי פִי אֶל־יְהוָה (Qal.Perf.1s פצה + pi + prep.+YHWH) — "I have opened my mouth unto YHWH" - וְלֹא אוּכַל לָשׁוּב (we + Neg + Qal.Impf.1s יכל + Qal.InfCon שוב) — "and I cannot go/turn back"

    Observation: Jephthah's own interpretation ("I cannot go back") reflects his subjective sense of vow-irrevocability. But Torah explicitly provides off-ramps he does not invoke: Lev 5:4-5 (rash-oath guilt-offering); Lev 27:1-8 (neder redemption by priestly valuation); Num 30 (father's annulment of daughter's vow — inapplicable since Jephthah is vower, not daughter; but Num 30:2 governs the man's own vow-obligation, which the Lev 5:4-5 pathway can still mitigate). "I cannot go back" = Jephthah's subjective conviction, not Torah's legal assessment.

    Judges 11:36 (Daughter's Consent)¶

    Jdg 11:36 And she said unto him, My father, [if] thou hast opened thy mouth unto the LORD, do to me according to that which hath proceeded out of thy mouth; forasmuch as the LORD hath taken vengeance for thee of thine enemies, [even] of the children of Ammon.

    Hebrew parsing: - עֲשֵׂה לִי (Qal.Impv.2ms עשׂה + prep.+1s) "do to me" — SAME verb (עשׂה) as 11:39 climax; neutral "do/make" not sacrificial - כַּאֲשֶׁר יָצָא מִפִּיךָ (per. + Qal.Perf.3ms יצא + prep.+2ms) "according as it went out from thy mouth" — Num 30:2 legal idiom ("all that proceedeth out of his mouth")

    Judges 11:37-38 (Daughter's Two-Month Mountain Mourning of bethulim)¶

    Jdg 11:37 And she said unto her father, Let this thing be done for me: let me alone two months, that I may go up and down upon the mountains, and bewail my virginity, I and my fellows. Jdg 11:38 And he said, Go. And he sent her away [for] two months: and she went with her companions, and bewailed her virginity upon the mountains.

    Hebrew parsing: - יֵעָשֶׂה (Verb.Niphal.Impf.3ms עשׂה) — "let it be done" (polite passive — daughter requesting, not commanding) - הַרְפֵּה מִמֶּנִּי (Hiphil.Impv.2ms רפה + prep+1s) — "let me alone / desist from me" - וְאֵלְכָה (Qal.Impf.1s cohortative) — "let me go" - וְיָרַדְתִּי (weqatal Qal.Perf.1s) — "and I will descend" - וְאֶבְכֶּה (weqatal Qal.Impf.1s) — "and I will weep" - עַל־בְּתוּלַי (prep+Noun.fp.Abs+1s בתולים H1331) — "for my virginity-state" - Repetition in v. 38: וַתֵּבְךְּ עַל־בְּתוּלֶיהָ (Qal.Wayq.3fs + bethulim+3fs) — "and she wept over her virginity" — narrator echoes daughter's own phrasing

    Library reference: word-studies/H1331-bthuwliym.md — abstract plural of condition; ALL 10 OT occurrences describe LIVING women's virginity-state, never a deceased woman's past status.
