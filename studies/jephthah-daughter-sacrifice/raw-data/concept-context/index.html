    ================================================================ CONCEPT-CONTEXT ANALYSIS Source: concept_context.py ================================================================

    === Jdg 11:31 === Concepts in verse: EXODUS: yatsa (H3318) — "that, cometh forth" [x3 tagging] REPENTANCE: shub (H7725) — "when I return" SACRIFICE: zebach/olah concept — H5930 ("for a burnt offering")

    Same chapter matches (Jdg 11): 11:3 (EXODUS), 11:8 (REPENTANCE), 11:9 (REPENTANCE), 11:13 (REPENTANCE), 11:34 (EXODUS — daughter coming out), 11:35 (REPENTANCE — "I cannot go back"), 11:36 (EXODUS — "proceeded out of thy mouth"), 11:39 (REPENTANCE — "she returned").

    Same book matches (top 10): Jdg 6:18 — Gideon asks angel to stay while he brings an offering Jdg 13:16 — angel of YHWH: "if thou wilt offer a burnt offering, offer it unto the LORD" (angel REFUSES self-sacrifice; commends sacrifice OF ANIMAL TO YHWH) Jdg 13:19 — Manoah's kid-with-meat-offering Jdg 13:23 — Manoah's wife: "If the LORD were pleased to kill us, he would not have received a burnt offering..." [inference: YHWH's acceptance of sacrifice = life-affirming, not death- demanding] Jdg 15:19 — Samson revived by water

    These nearby-book concepts cluster around SACRIFICE + DELIVERANCE motifs. Jdg 13:16-23 is especially instructive: the angel of YHWH does NOT permit personal-sacrifice and redirects to animal burnt-offering — the exact opposite of what a literal Jdg 11:39 would require.

    === Jdg 11:39 === Concepts in verse: TELOS: qets (H7093) — "at the end" REPENTANCE: shub (H7725) — "she returned" FULFILLMENT: asah (H6213) — "who did" (neutral verb, unlike olah/ zabach/saraph/ha'evir ba'esh) LAW: choq (H2706) — "a custom/statute"

    Same chapter matches: 11:10 (FULFILLMENT — elders' pledge to Jephthah), 11:13, 11:27 ("the LORD the Judge"), 11:31, 11:35, 11:36.

    Same book matches: Jdg 17:3 — Micah's mother dedicates silver "wholly unto the LORD" then makes graven image [FULFILLMENT + REPENTANCE clustering with dedicated-vow-gone-irregular — structural parallel to Jephthah's Torah-irregular vow] Jdg 17:4 — idem Jdg 2:17 — apostasy refrain (LAW) Jdg 21:15 — Israel repented of Benjamin-vow (rash oath + national recovery) Jdg 21:23 — Benjamin-virgin recovery (Benjamin's inheritance preserved via a VIRGIN-acquisition procedure — same lexical field as Jdg 11 daughter) Jdg 7:17 — Gideon's "so shall ye do"

    Same author (Historical): Neh 1:9, 9:26, 9:29 — covenant/return/commandment cluster 1 Chr 16:12 — "remember his marvellous works" [FULFILLMENT] Ezra 9:14 — "break thy commandments" [LAW + REPENTANCE]

    === Lev 27:2 === Concepts found: LIFE (nefesh H5315 — "the persons")

    Same-book matches: only Lev 11 food-law verses tagged under LIFE (semantic-classifier gap — the priestly vow-law did not match other priestly contexts on concepts).

    Same-author matches (Moses): Deut 10:12, 10:22, 11:13, 11:18 — all "heart and soul" covenant fidelity texts. The semantic-classifier associates the vow-of-persons regulation with whole-life devotion, not sacrificial death.

    Other books: 1 Chr 11:19 — David's rejection of blood-of-men offering ("shall I drink the blood of these men that have put their lives in jeopardy?") — STRONG conceptual parallel: David recoils from symbolic- drinking of life-endangered men's sacrifice. Also 1 Chr 22:19 — "set your heart and soul to seek the LORD" + 1 Chr 28:9 + 2 Chr 17:16 (Zichri's self-consecration). The Chronicler's theology = person- dedication is SOUL-SERVICE, not death-offering.

    === 1 Sam 1:11 (Hannah) === Concepts in verse: PROPHET: ra'ah/naba (semantic field) — "thou wilt, look, indeed" LIFE: chay (H2416) — "of his life" [kol yemei CHAYYAV]

    Same-chapter: 1 Sam 1:10 (LIFE — "bitterness of soul"), 1:15 ("poured out my soul"), 1:22 ("abide for ever"), 1:26 ("thy soul liveth"). ALL LIFE- related. Hannah's vow-frame is entirely in the LIFE register, not the SACRIFICE register.

    Same-book: 1 Sam 10:24 Samuel anoints Saul; 1 Sam 17:55 David chosen; 1 Sam 19:5 David's "life in hand" Philistine victory; 1 Sam 23:15 David pursued.

    Same-author (Historical): 1 Kgs 1:34 Zadok/Nathan anointing; 1 Kgs 17:23 Elijah raises widow's son ("thy son liveth"); 1 Kgs 18:15, 19:10 ("LORD of hosts liveth"). The book's vocabulary of dedication-to-YHWH is paired with LIVING-service, never with burnt-offering immolation.

    ================================================================ KEY SEMANTIC-CLASSIFIER OBSERVATION ================================================================

    The concept_context.py classifier independently tagged: - Jdg 11:31 (vow) with SACRIFICE (because of olah) - Jdg 11:39 (fulfillment) with FULFILLMENT (neutral asah) — NOT SACRIFICE - 1 Sam 1:11 (Hannah) with LIFE (because of chay) - Lev 27:2 with LIFE (because of nefesh)

    That the fulfillment-verse (Jdg 11:39) sheds the SACRIFICE tag that the vow-verse (Jdg 11:31) carries is grammatically and lexically significant. The narrator's verb-choice (עשׂה asah vs. עלה Hiphil + olah) registers on automatic semantic classifiers.
